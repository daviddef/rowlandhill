## 2. The important finding of this session

**The low country coverage was our bug, not a thin source.**

`deep_harvest.py` descended only into Commons subcategories with a year in the name:
```python
return bool(YEAR_RE.search(sub)) or "by year" in sub.lower()
```
Norway files as `1949 stamps of Norway` â†’ matches. Afghanistan files as `Postage stamps of
Afghanistan by date` (**by date**, not by year) plus `Airmail stamps ofâ€¦` â†’ no match, so descent
stopped at the root and it kept only the 2 loose files. We held exactly 2.

It hid because the symptom looked exactly like a thin source: coverage correlated with country
size, which is plausible enough that nobody checked.

Fixed in `scripts/deep_harvest_v2.py`. Measured:

| | before | after |
|---|---|---|
| Afghanistan | 2 | **1,485** |
| Sri Lanka | 3 | 82 |
| Ghana | 12 | 81 |
| Bangladesh | 3 | 13 |

**Lesson worth keeping:** the plan was to deep-research each country's philatelic history. That
would have produced a well-sourced essay on Afghanistan and missed the answer entirely, which
was a regex. Measure our own pipeline before researching the world.
