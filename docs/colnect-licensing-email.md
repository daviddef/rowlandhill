**Subject:** Commercial data-licensing enquiry — stamp identification app

Hello Colnect team,

I'm building **Rowland**, an iOS app that identifies postage stamps from a photo and helps
collectors catalogue and value their collections. It's in active development, pre-launch.

I'd like to explore **licensing Colnect's stamp catalogue data** for use in the app. Colnect is
clearly the most complete philatelic catalogue available, and rather than rebuild what you've
spent years compiling, I'd much prefer to work with you.

Specifically, I'm interested in understanding:

1. **Commercial licensing terms** for the Collector API (CAPI) — is there a standard licence
   for a commercial mobile app, and what does it cost (flat fee, subscription, revenue share)?
2. **What the licence covers** — stamp records (issuer, year, denomination, catalogue numbers,
   descriptions), images, and cross-catalogue references (Scott / Stanley Gibbons / Michel /
   Yvert). Are catalogue-number cross-references included, or licensed separately?
3. **Access and limits** — request rate limits, bulk export options, and update cadence.
4. **Attribution** — how you'd like Colnect credited in the app.

If it's easier to discuss on a call, I'm happy to arrange one. I want to build this the right
way, with a proper commercial relationship rather than scraping, and I'd value your guidance on
how you normally work with app developers.

Thank you for your time — I look forward to hearing from you.

## Notes for David (delete before sending)

- **Keep it non-committal on price.** You're asking what it costs, not agreeing to anything.
- **The cross-reference question (point 2) is the important one.** If Colnect can license the
  Scott/SG/Michel numbers as part of the data, that likely resolves the numbering-rights
  question in one deal — you get both the records *and* the legal cover for the catalogue
  numbers that are our stated core IP. Ask the IP lawyer whether a Colnect licence covers you,
  or whether the numbers still need separate permission from Scott/SG/Michel themselves.
- **Do not mention scraping**, even to say you won't — it plants the wrong idea. The draft
  frames it positively ("rather than rebuild… I'd prefer to work with you").
- Colnect's own terms say API access needs prior written permission and is revocable, so
  getting a written licence is exactly the right move.
