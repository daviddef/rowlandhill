## 2. First issue

| Field | Value | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| **Date** | **19 June 1878** | High — consistently given by Wikipedia and multiple dealer/reference sources. Not merely "1878". |
| **Catalogue number** | **SG 1** = 1d claret. Scott 1 = the 1d. | High |
| **Values in the very first release** | **1d and 6d only** | High |
| **Subject** | Queen Victoria, profile facing **right**, in an oval with pearl ornament surround | High |
| **Printer** | **Bradbury, Wilkinson & Co. Ltd** | **Medium-High — and the claim survives checking** (see below) |
| **Process** | Recess (line-engraved) | Medium |
| **Perforation** | Line perf 14 × 14½ | Medium |
| **Paper** | **Unwatermarked** — unusual for a British colony; corrected to watermarked paper in 1883 | High |

**On the printer claim (the task flagged this as often misstated) — verdict: it holds up.**
Bradbury Wilkinson is *independently* attested for the 1878 Falklands first issue by more than one
source, and is separately attested for later Falklands work (the recess high values that became the
template for all Edward VII / George V high values through 1929, and the 1937–49 KGVI set). The
common way this gets garbled in dealer copy is the reverse error — attributing the *1878* issue to
De La Rue by analogy with most other Victorian colonial issues. **No source found supports De La Rue
for 1878.** Residual caution: I could not read a Stanley Gibbons catalogue header directly, which
would be the definitive confirmation. Rated Medium-High, not certain.

**Myth watch:** several dealer/aggregator pages describe "the 1878 set" as **four values (1d, 4d, 6d,
1s)**. That is the **1878–79 series as a whole**, not the 19 June 1878 first day. The 4d and 1s were
added later. Do not record "1878: four values issued" as a single event.

## 3. Issuing periods

| Era | Dates | Issuing authority | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-adhesive / pre-stamp | to 1878 | British colonial administration | Falklands applied to join the General Postal Union from Oct 1877 (Earl of Carnarvon, Colonial Office) — the proximate trigger for issuing stamps. |
| Crown colony issues | 1878 – 1981 | **Falkland Islands (British Crown colony)** | Continuous colonial issuing. Long thin patch 1902–1927 (see Gaps). |
| Dependencies overprints | 1944 | Falkland Islands colonial govt, on behalf of the **Dependencies** | 4 territorial overprint sets. |
| Dependencies general issues | 1946 – 1962 | **Falkland Islands Dependencies** | Inscribed FALKLAND ISLANDS DEPENDENCIES. |
| Dependencies post-BAT | 1963 – 1985 | **Falkland Islands Dependencies** (reduced to South Georgia + South Sandwich) | With a hiatus; issues resume 1980. |
| **1982 Argentine occupation** | **2 Apr – 14 Jun 1982** | **Argentina — see verdict below** | |
| British Dependent Territory | 1981 – 2002 | Falkland Islands Government | Status renamed; issuing unbroken. |
| Philatelic Bureau era | 1978 – present | Falkland Islands Philatelic Bureau | Bureau established **1978** — the inflection point for issue volume. |
| British Overseas Territory | 2002 – present | Falkland Islands Government | |
| Privatised postal operator | **1 Aug 2014** – present | **Falklands Post Service Ltd** | Operates the post and the philatelic bureau. **Crucially: the Falkland Islands Government retains the Post Master role and retains responsibility for postage rates and for the design and value of all stamps and philatelic products.** This is the fact that keeps Falklands out of the agency-wallpaper category — see §7. |

### Did Argentina issue stamps for the occupied islands? — verified answer

**No separate stamps were issued *for* the Falkland Islands by Argentina.** What actually happened:

1. **Argentine stamps of Argentina** were used on the islands during the occupation, cancelled with
   postmarks inscribed **"ISLAS MALVINAS"** (Puerto Argentino was the renamed Stanley). These are
   collected as **Argentine postal history / Falklands postal history**, not as a Falklands
   catalogue country.
2. **Argentina issued its own commemoratives** on the Malvinas theme in 1982 (one source describes an
   overprint reading *"Las Malvinas son Argentinas"*). These are catalogued under **Argentina** —
   they are Argentine stamps about the islands, not island stamps.

**Practical consequence for a base catalogue:** there is **no "1982 Argentine occupation of the
Falkland Islands" issuing authority**. Any dealer listing offering "Argentine occupation stamps of
the Falkland Islands" as a distinct issuing entity is misdescribing either (a) Argentine stamps with
Islas Malvinas cancels, or (b) Argentine home issues. *Uncertain:* the exact Argentine catalogue
numbers of the 1982 Malvinas issues, and whether a genuine overprint (as opposed to a
newly-designed stamp) was involved — the wording of that one source was not independently corroborated.

## 4. Predecessor / successor issuers — disentangling the South Atlantic

This is the highest-risk area for catalogue modelling. Five distinct catalogue countries are involved.

| Catalogue country (exact name) | Existed as separate catalogue country | SG numbering | Scott prefix |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Falkland Islands** | 1878 – present (unbroken) | 1 – ~1585+ | plain (Scott 1– ) |
| **Falkland Islands Dependencies** | 1944 – 1985 | letter-prefixed: **A/B/C/D** (1944 overprints), **G** (1946 map issue) | **1L** |
| **Graham Land** | 1944 only | **SG A1–A8** | **2L** |
| **South Georgia** (as Dependencies overprint, 1944) | 1944 | **SG B1–B8** | **3L** |
| **South Orkneys** | 1944 only | **SG C1–C8** *(inferred — see caveat)* | **4L** |
| **South Shetlands** | 1944 only | **SG D1–D8** | **5L** |
| **South Georgia** (own country, post-BAT) | **1963 – 1980** | restarts at SG 1 (e.g. 1963–69 = SG 1–16) | separate |
| **British Antarctic Territory** | **1963 – present** | restarts at SG 1 | separate |
| **South Georgia and the South Sandwich Islands** | **1985 – present** | restarts at SG 1 | separate |

### The chronological story

- **1908/1917–1944**: the Dependencies (South Georgia, South Orkneys, South Shetlands, Graham Land)
  are administered from Stanley but **issue nothing of their own** — Falkland Islands stamps are used.
- **1944 (5 February for at least the South Shetlands set)**: eight Falkland Islands stamps from the
  1938 and 1941 printings are overprinted **in red** with each dependency's name. The wartime motive
  was assertion of British sovereignty (Operation Tabarin), not postal need.
- **1946**: first general issues **inscribed "FALKLAND ISLANDS DEPENDENCIES"** — the *map* definitives.
- **1963**: **British Antarctic Territory** is created, taking the Antarctic mainland and the
  South Orkneys/South Shetlands out of the Dependencies. **South Georgia** begins issuing under its
  own name (SG 1– ). The Dependencies name goes quiet.
- **1980**: Dependencies issues resume briefly.
- **1985**: the Dependencies are superseded by **South Georgia and the South Sandwich Islands**, which
  becomes the ongoing catalogue country.

### "Where would a collector find the 1944 overprints?"

**Two different answers depending on catalogue — this is the trap.**

- **Stanley Gibbons**: all four 1944 overprint sets are listed **under Falkland Islands Dependencies**,
  as lettered sub-series (A = Graham Land, B = South Georgia, C = South Orkneys, D = South Shetlands),
  sitting immediately before the G-prefixed 1946 general issues. A collector looks under *Falkland
  Islands Dependencies*, not under "Graham Land".
- **Scott**: the individual territories are listed as **separate sub-countries following Falkland
  Islands Dependencies**, with their own numeric prefixes (1L = Dependencies general, 2L = Graham
  Land, 3L = South Georgia, 4L = South Orkneys, 5L = South Shetlands). A collector looks under
  *Graham Land* / *South Orkney Islands* as named headings.

So **"Graham Land" and "South Shetlands" are real catalogue country names in Scott but only sub-series
labels in SG.** A base catalogue must decide which convention it follows and should probably carry
both as aliases.

**Caveat on SG C1–C8 for South Orkneys:** A1–A8 (Graham Land), B-prefix (South Georgia, verified via
SG B6) and D1–D8 (South Shetlands) are each directly attested in sources read. **C1–C8 for South
Orkneys is inferred by elimination from the A/B/D pattern** and was not directly confirmed. Verify
against a printed SG catalogue before relying on it.

### The 1946 Dependencies "map" issues

- Inscribed **FALKLAND ISLANDS DEPENDENCIES**, King George VI portrait with a map of the Dependencies.
- Two printings, universally called **"thick map"** and **"thin map"** — the map engraving differs.
- **SG G1–G8** is the map set. *Uncertain:* which printing SG G1–G8 covers. Sources conflict directly:
  one dealer catalogues "1946-49 **Thick** Maps Set SG G1–G8", while a Colnect-derived listing gives
  Type I = 1946-02-01 and Type II = 1948-02-16, and another source states the **thick** set ran from
  **12 July 1946** and the **thin** set was introduced in **1948** and is the more valuable.
  **Do not record a first-day date for the 1946 map issue** — 1 Feb 1946 and 12 July 1946 are both in
  circulation and I could not adjudicate. The thin/thick → G1–G8 / G9–G16 mapping is likewise
  *Uncertain*.
- Well-known constant plate varieties exist on this issue (e.g. "gap in 80th parallel", SG G5a/G6a),
  which is why the set is heavily specialised.

## 5. Inscriptions — the text printed on the stamps

| Inscription (as printed) | Used on | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **FALKLAND ISLANDS** | 1878 – present, all Falkland Islands proper issues | The constant. |
| **FALKLAND ISLANDS DEPENDENCIES** | 1946 – 1985 general issues | The 1946 map definitives onward. |
| **GRAHAM LAND / DEPENDENCY OF** | 1944 overprint on Falkland Islands stamps | Overprint in **red**. Base stamps remain inscribed FALKLAND ISLANDS — the overprint is *additional* text. |
| **SOUTH GEORGIA / DEPENDENCY OF** | 1944 overprint | red |
| **SOUTH ORKNEYS / DEPENDENCY OF** | 1944 overprint | red |
| **SOUTH SHETLANDS / DEPENDENCY OF** | 1944 overprint | red; verified wording |
| **SOUTH GEORGIA** | 1963–1980 own-country issues | |
| **SOUTH GEORGIA AND THE SOUTH SANDWICH ISLANDS** | 1985 – present | |
| **BRITISH ANTARCTIC TERRITORY** | 1963 – present | |
| **1833 / 1983** (dual date) | 1983 commemorative set | 150th anniversary of British administration. |

**OCR / recognition note:** the 1944 overprints are the only case where a stamp carries **two
conflicting country inscriptions simultaneously** — "FALKLAND ISLANDS" printed and e.g. "SOUTH
SHETLANDS DEPENDENCY OF" overprinted. Any automatic country attribution from inscription text must
prioritise the overprint. The exact line-break order ("SOUTH SHETLANDS" above "DEPENDENCY OF" vs the
reverse) is reported as *"SOUTH SHETLANDS / DEPENDENCY OF"* by the sources read, but the physical
layout on the stamp was not visually verified — mark *Uncertain*.

## 6. Gaps — years with no issues

| Period | Status | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **1902 – 1927** (approximately) | **Long thin period, near-certainly containing gap years** | Two causes. (a) The 1878-design values were added only "from time to time until 1902", after which new issues were sparse. (b) The **opening of the Panama Canal (1914) removed the Cape Horn shipping route**, and **regular mail service to the Falklands was not resumed until 1927**. *Uncertain:* exactly which calendar years have zero issues — I could not obtain a clean year-by-year list (Colnect and StampWorld both blocked automated access). |
| Scattered pre-1900 | Gaps present | A catalogue listing shows the sequence jumping 1891 → 1898 in its series list, implying no new *series* in 1892–97. Individual printings/shades may still exist. |
| **1982 — the conflict year** | **NOT a gap. Do not record it as one.** | The islands were occupied 2 April – 14 June 1982, but the Falkland Islands issuing programme continued. Argentine stamps circulated *during* the ~10-week occupation; that did not cancel the year's British Falklands programme. |
| 1978 onward | No gaps | With the Philatelic Bureau established 1978, issues become annual and multiple-per-year without interruption through 2026. |

**Myth corrected — the "1982 set":** at least one aggregator states that **SG 439–449, the 11-value
150th Anniversary of British Administration set, is a 1982 issue.** It is **1983** (1833 + 150 = 1983;
independently confirmed by a dealer listing dated 1 January 1983 and by the "1833/1983" inscription).
The 1982-vs-1983 slip appears to arise from AI-assembled dealer copy conflating the war year with the
anniversary set. **Do not record SG 439–449 as 1982.**

## 7. Forgery risk and wallpaper risk

### Wallpaper risk: **LOW. Falkland Islands is NOT an agency wallpaper issuer. State this plainly.**

The reasoning, not just the conclusion:

- The **Falkland Islands Government retains the Post Master role and retains control of postage rates
  and of the design and value of all stamps and philatelic products** — even after postal operations
  were privatised to Falklands Post Service Ltd on 1 Aug 2014. Editorial control has never been sold
  or licensed to an outside philatelic agency. This is the structural test for wallpaper status, and
  Falklands passes it.
- Issue volume is modest and thematically local: the 2024 programme was seven issues (Working Dogs,
  St Mary's Church 125th, a memorial to two firemen, Macaroni Penguin, Shackleton's *Endurance*,
  Churchill 150th, Stanley sights). Subjects are overwhelmingly local — wildlife, local history,
  Antarctic exploration, local institutions. This is the profile of a real post office serving a real
  (if tiny) population plus a genuine collector base, not of an agency printing Disney and Diana.
- The Churchill 150th is the only obviously outward-facing subject in 2024 and is a legitimate
  Commonwealth-wide theme.

**Nuance, stated honestly:** with a resident population around 3,500, the *revenue* motive of Falklands
philately is unmistakably collector-facing — the Philatelic Bureau exists precisely to sell to
collectors. But "issues stamps partly for collectors" is true of nearly every Commonwealth territory
and is not what "wallpaper" means. Wallpaper means an outside agency controlling the programme and
producing issues with no postal reality. That is not the Falklands.

### Forgery risk: **HIGH for pre-1930 material and for the 1944 overprints.**

Verified forgery targets:

| Target | Nature of forgery | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **1878 QV 6d** | Multiple full stamp forgeries, with design differences from the genuine | stampforgeries.com |
| **1891 QV provisional surcharges** | Forged surcharges on genuine stamps; one documented with a **1943 RPSL certificate** | stampforgeries.com; SG |
| **1891 QV bisect (½d)** | Forgeries of both SG 13 and SG 14; the 1d was diagonally bisected, used as-is or overprinted ½d — **both variants have been forged**. **Genuine surcharges are usually clearly handstamped with a relatively large stop/period** — a usable diagnostic. | stampforgeries.com |
| **1912 KGV** | **Madame Joseph forged postmarks** (the classic faked-cancel operation, active on used-value British colonials) | stampforgeries.com |
| **1928 KGV surcharge** | Forged overprint on a genuine stamp | stampforgeries.com |
| Various | **Raoul Ch. de Thuin** surcharge counterfeits; "Gee-Ma" forgeries noted in Robson Lowe's research | stampforgeries.com |

**The 1944 Dependencies overprints — the structural risk.** These are eight *cheap and common*
Falkland Islands stamps made valuable purely by a **red overprint**. That is the single most forgeable
configuration in philately: the base stamp is genuine and inexpensive, and the entire premium sits in
a few words of red ink that a competent forger can apply. The **6d value is noted as by far the most
valuable mint stamp in the set and rarely found unmounted** — precisely the value a forger would
target. Certification is effectively mandatory for high-value examples.

**Honest gap:** despite targeted searching I found **no source specifically documenting confirmed
forgeries of the 1944 Dependencies overprints**. The risk above is inferred from the economics of the
issue, not evidenced. Stated as risk, not as fact.

**Best specialist reference located:** `novastamps.com/F-I-Forgeries.pdf` — *"FALKLAND ISLANDS
Forgeries and Fakes of Stamps, Surcharges and Postmarks"*, hosted on the site of **Stefan Heijtz**, a
recognised Falklands specialist. Not fetched in this pass; **this should be the first document read in
any follow-up.**

## 8. Image sourcing

**Wikimedia Commons — the task's premise needs correcting: Commons has *some* Falklands stamp images,
though very few.**

`Category:Stamps of the Falkland Islands` contains:
- 2 files directly (`Falkland Islands stamps.jpg` at 5102×7208, and `1906 cover Falkland Islands.jpg`)
- 4 subcategories: *Stamps by year* (20 items), *Stamps of Dependencies* (9 items),
  *Elizabeth II on stamps* (2 files), *George VI on stamps* (3 files)

So roughly **35–40 items total, mostly low-value**, and **no systematic per-issue coverage**. The
practical conclusion the task was reaching for is correct: **Commons cannot supply a usable image set
for a Falklands base catalogue.** *Uncertain:* the licence tag on each individual file — Commons
holdings of colonial stamps are a mix of PD-old, PD-UKGov, and occasional errors. **Each file must be
licence-checked individually; do not bulk-ingest.**

### Copyright status

| Aspect | Position | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| Rights holder, modern issues | Falkland Islands Government (retains design responsibility even post-privatisation) | High |
| Rights holder, colonial-era issues | Crown copyright, most plausibly vested in the colonial government | *Uncertain* — not verified |
| **UK Crown copyright term** | **50 years from the end of the year of first publication**, for published works | Medium — this is the standard UK rule but was **not verified against a source in this pass** |
| Practical consequence if the 50-year rule applies | Everything published **up to and including 1975** would be out of Crown copyright; **1976–present would still be protected** | Conditional on the above |
| Does UK Crown copyright law even extend to the Falklands? | **Genuinely uncertain.** The Falkland Islands is a separate jurisdiction with its own legislature and may have its own copyright ordinance with a different term. **Do not assume UK law applies.** | Low |
| Open Government Licence applicability | **Unverified and probably inapplicable** — OGL covers UK central government, not Overseas Territory governments | Low |

**Recommended sourcing route:** for pre-1976 material, Commons plus PD-old scans, licence-checked file
by file. For modern issues, **there is no reusable-licensed source** — the realistic path is a direct
permission request to **Falklands Post Service Ltd / the Falkland Islands Philatelic Bureau**, or
licensed catalogue imagery. Dealer-site scans (john-rice-stamps, commonwealthstamps, bcstamps,
grahamlandstamps) are **all rights-reserved and must not be scraped.**

## 9. Uncertain — stated generously

Things this dossier does **not** establish:

1. **Scott and Michel headline counts.** No figure found. Do not populate.
2. **Exact current SG highest number.** SG 1581–1584 is a verified late-2024 anchor; everything past
   that is projection.
3. **Precise year-by-year gap list, 1902–1927.** The narrative cause (Panama Canal 1914, mail service
   not resumed until 1927) is solid; the specific zero-issue calendar years are not.
4. **The 1946 map issue first-day date.** Sources give both 1 Feb 1946 and 12 July 1946. Unresolved.
5. **Which of SG G1–G8 / G9–G16 is thick vs thin map.** Sources conflict.
6. **SG C1–C8 for the South Orkneys 1944 overprints.** Inferred from the A/B/D pattern, not confirmed.
7. **Exact overprint line layout** on the 1944 sets (which line sits above which).
8. **The 1878 printer at catalogue-header level.** Bradbury Wilkinson is well-attested in secondary
   sources and no contrary attribution was found, but a printed SG catalogue was not consulted.
9. **Whether Argentina's 1982 Malvinas issue was an overprint or a new design**, and its Argentine
   catalogue numbers.
10. **Copyright term and jurisdiction for Falkland Islands stamp designs.** The single weakest section
    here. Requires a lawyer or a Falkland Islands copyright ordinance, not a web search.
11. **Confirmed forgeries of the 1944 Dependencies overprints.** Risk is inferred from issue economics;
    no documented forgery found.
12. **Whether Colnect country id 9252 is a duplicate** of id 70 or a distinct listing.
13. **Falkland Islands Dependencies and BAT/SGSSI total counts.**
14. **Exact date the 1944 overprints were issued** for territories other than the South Shetlands
    (5 Feb 1944 verified for South Shetlands only).

### Myths flagged in this dossier

- ❌ "The 1878 first issue was four values." → It was **1d and 6d**; 4d and 1s came later in 1878–79.
- ❌ "SG 439–449 is a 1982 issue." → **1983** (150th anniversary of British administration, 1833→1983).
- ❌ "Argentina issued Falkland Islands stamps during the 1982 occupation." → Argentina's *own* stamps
  were used with **"ISLAS MALVINAS"** postmarks; Argentine Malvinas commemoratives are catalogued
  under **Argentina**. There is no Falklands-country occupation issue.
- ❌ "The 1878 issue was printed by De La Rue." → No source supports this; **Bradbury Wilkinson** is
  the attested printer.
- ⚠️ "Wikimedia Commons has no Falklands stamp images." → It has ~35–40, but they are unsystematic and
  individually licence-variable, so the operational conclusion (unusable as a catalogue image source)
  stands even though the literal claim is wrong.
