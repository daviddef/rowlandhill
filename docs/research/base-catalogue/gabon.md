# Gabon

Base-catalogue skeleton dossier. Compiled 2026-07-19 from web sources. Every count is tagged
with the catalogue it came from; untagged counts are worthless because catalogues disagree
by roughly ±20% on major numbers (differing treatment of se-tenant sheets, miniature sheets,
colour/paper varieties, and airmail/postage-due sub-series).

| Scope | Catalogue | Approx. major numbers | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gabon, all periods, all functions | Yvert et Tellier (via Colnect index) | ~1,775 | Medium — Colnect's tally of items it has mapped to a Y&T number, not Y&T's own printed total |
| Gabon, all periods, sets | Freestampcatalogue (Michel/Yvert mixed) | 806 *sets* (not individual stamps) | Low — set-level, not comparable to a stamp count |
| Gabon classic era only, 1886–1933 (regular + semi-postal + postage due) | Scott Classic Specialized (2011 ed.) | 183 major numbers | High — explicit figure |
| French Equatorial Africa 1936–1958 (the period during which Gabon issued nothing of its own) | StampWorld | 312 postage stamps | Medium |
| Gabon, StampWorld country range | StampWorld | listed 1886–2019 (count not retrieved, site 403s to automated fetch) | Low |

**Working headline figure: roughly 1,600–1,900 major numbers for Gabon across all periods,
per Yvert et Tellier.** The wide band reflects (a) whether the AEF period is folded in,
(b) whether the large 1960s–1990s airmail series are counted separately, and (c) how many
of the post-2000 "issues" a given catalogue refuses to list at all (see §7).

**Why Yvert et Tellier is the authority here.** Y&T is the French national catalogue and
publishes a dedicated *Timbres des Colonies Françaises et Territoires* volume. French colonial
postal administration records — decrees, printing orders from the Paris government printing
works, distribution circulars — are French-language archival material that Y&T has worked from
continuously since the 19th century. Scott and Gibbons both, in practice, derive French
colonial listings from Y&T and then renumber. For dating disputes, overprint-variety
recognition, and which provisional handstamps are "real", Y&T is the source of record;
Scott is the source of record for the *English-language* number that most Anglophone
software and dealers key on. A catalogue-agnostic database should store **both**.

## 2. First issue

- **Date: 31 July 1886.** This date is consistent across Wikipedia and the Big Blue
  1840–1940 survey. Treat as high confidence.
- **Nature:** *not* a printed Gabon stamp. It was a set of handstamped surcharges applied
  locally to French Colonies general-issue stock (Type Alphée Dubois) to cover shortages of
  the commonest values. The handstamp reads **"GAB"** rendered in dots, plus a new value
  (GAB 5, GAB 25, GAB 50, GAB 75 are the recorded values).
- **Catalogue numbering:** Scott begins Gabon at Scott 1. Mystic lists a "2 - 1886 Gabon".
  The 1886–1889 handstamped/surcharged group totals **15 stamps** in Scott. The precise
  Scott 1–15 to Y&T concordance was **not verified** — see §9.
- **Subject depicted:** the underlying design is the French Colonies "Commerce"/Alphée Dubois
  allegorical type. There is no Gabon-specific imagery in the first issue at all.
- **Printer:** the underlying stamps were printed in Paris by the French government stamp
  printing works (Atelier/Imprimerie des Timbres-Poste). The **surcharge itself was applied
  locally in Gabon by handstamp** — so "printer" is only meaningfully answerable for the
  basic stamp, not the provisional.
- **1888–1889 follow-ons:** 1888 surcharges are the bare numeral. 1889 postage dues were
  overprinted **"GABON / TIMBRE"** to convert them to ordinary postage. Also 1889, locally
  typeset 15c and 25c inscribed **"Gabon-Congo / POSTES"** with a diagonal
  "Republique Française".

### First *printed* Gabon issue — and a myth to kill

- **1904–07: the "Navigation and Commerce" (French: *type Groupe*) issue, 17 values,
  1c to 5fr.** Designed by **Louis-Eugène Mouchon**, typographed, printed in Paris; the
  colony name sits in a rectangular cartouche at the foot of the design. Scott numbering
  for this group runs from roughly Scott 16; Big Blue cites Scott 16–18, 19 within a
  17-stamp group.
- **MYTH FLAG — "Faidherbe type" is NOT a Gabon issue.** The brief for this dossier paired
  "Navigation and Commerce"/Faidherbe. They are different things and Faidherbe does not
  belong to Gabon. General Louis Faidherbe was Governor of **Senegal**; the Faidherbe
  portrait type appears on **Senegal** stamps from 1906 (and related Senegambia/Upper Senegal
  areas), not Gabon. Any dealer page or AI-written listing attributing a "Faidherbe type"
  to Gabon is wrong. Gabon's 1904–07 first printed issue is Navigation and Commerce only.

## 3. Issuing periods

| Period | Dates | Issuing authority | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-stamp / French Colonies general issues | 1862 – 31 Jul 1886 | French colonial post, Libreville office (opened 1862) | Mail used French Colonies general issues; "GAB" was a *cancellation*, not an overprint. Routed via the British post office on Fernando Pó. |
| Gabon provisionals | 31 Jul 1886 – 1889 | Colony of Gabon, French Ministry of Marine/Colonies | Local handstamps and local typeset issues |
| French Congo (Congo Français) | 1891 – 1904 | Colony of French Congo | **Gabon issued nothing of its own.** Material of these years is catalogued under **French Congo**, a separate catalogue country. |
| Gabon as separate colony | 1904 – 1933 | Colony of Gabon (within AEF federation from 1910) | Navigation & Commerce 1904–07; pictorial definitives 1910; AEF overprints 1924–31; final pictorial definitives 1932–33 |
| French Equatorial Africa (AEF) | 1936 – 1958 | Gouvernement général de l'Afrique Équatoriale Française, Brazzaville | Gabon-inscribed issues cease. Catalogued under **French Equatorial Africa**. Includes the Free French / Vichy split (see below). |
| Free French / Vichy overlay | c. 1940 – 1944 | AEF administration under Free France ("France Libre") from Aug 1940 | AEF declared for de Gaulle in Aug 1940; Gabon itself was taken from Vichy control in the Battle of Gabon, Nov 1940. Overprinted "FRANCE LIBRE" / "AFRIQUE FRANÇAISE LIBRE" material exists under AEF. **Exact overprint inventory not verified — see §9.** |
| Autonomous Republic (French Community) | Republic proclaimed 28 Nov 1958; first own stamps 28 Nov 1959 | République Gabonaise, within the French Community | Between Nov 1958 and Nov 1959 the country used AEF stamps |
| Independent Gabon | 17 Aug 1960 – present | République Gabonaise; postal operator historically the **Office des Postes et Télécommunications (OPT)**, later restructured into **La Poste Gabon / Poste Gabon S.A.** | Operator naming across the restructurings is **not fully verified** — see §9 |

## 4. Predecessor / successor issuers (catalogue country names)

Chain, using names a catalogue would actually print as a country heading:

1. **French Colonies (general issues)** — pre-1886 usage in Gabon; Scott/Y&T "French Colonies".
2. **Gabon** — 1886–1889 provisionals.
3. **French Congo** (*Congo Français*) — 1891–1904. **Gabon material of these years is filed here, not under Gabon.**
4. **Gabon** — 1904–1933.
5. **Middle Congo** (*Moyen-Congo*) — a *sibling*, not a predecessor, of Gabon within AEF; matters because the 1936 AEF issues were overprints on **both** Gabon stamps (10 stamps) and Middle Congo stamps (16 stamps). A Gabon stamp overprinted for AEF is catalogued under **French Equatorial Africa**, not Gabon.
6. **French Equatorial Africa** (*Afrique Équatoriale Française* / AEF) — 1936–1958. This entity **subsumed** Gabon's issues. AEF split in 1958 into four republics: Chad, Congo, Gabon, Central African Republic.
7. **Gabon** (République Gabonaise) — 1959 onward, continuing through independence in 1960 with no catalogue-country break at independence.

Sibling AEF constituents whose stamps are frequently mis-sold as Gabon: **Middle Congo**,
**Ubangi-Shari** (*Oubangui-Chari*), **Chad** (*Tchad*).

## 5. Inscriptions — the identification field

Listed by the text actually printed on the stamp.

| Printed inscription | Period | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| No country inscription; handstamp **"GAB"** in dots + numeral | 1886 | Underlying stamp is French Colonies "Commerce" type. Overprint only. |
| Numeral surcharge only | 1888 | No country text at all — attribution depends on provenance and the surcharge type |
| **"GABON / TIMBRE"** | 1889 | Overprint converting postage dues |
| **"Gabon-Congo / POSTES"** with diagonal **"Republique Française"** | 1889 | Locally typeset 15c and 25c |
| **"GABON"** in the foot cartouche, with **"RF"** and **"POSTES"** in the Navigation and Commerce frame | 1904–1907 | The classic *type Groupe* layout |
| **"CONGO FRANÇAIS / GABON"** | 1910 pictorial definitives | Fang Warrior, Libreville, Fang Woman designs. Inscription is a leftover: French Congo had already been reorganised. This dual inscription is the single sharpest date marker in the whole country. |
| **"AFRIQUE ÉQUATORIALE / GABON"** (combined) | c. 1910–1922, same three designs re-inscribed | The re-inscription of the 1910 designs. Still a **Gabon** catalogue country. Do not confuse with §AEF below. |
| Overprint **"AFRIQUE ÉQUATORIALE FRANÇAISE"** on Gabon-inscribed stamps | 1924–1931 | Overprint on Gabon stamps; **still catalogued under Gabon** in Scott |
| **"GABON"** + **"AFRIQUE ÉQUATORIALE FRANÇAISE"** | 1932–1933 definitives | Ogooué timber raft, de Brazza, Setta Kemma village |
| **"AFRIQUE ÉQUATORIALE FRANÇAISE"** and/or **"A.E.F."**, *no* Gabon | 1936–1958 | Catalogued under **French Equatorial Africa** |
| **"RÉPUBLIQUE GABONAISE"** | 1959 – present | The standard modern inscription. Wikipedia-derived source states "All stamps have REPUBLIQUE GABONAISE in the frank" for the republic era. |
| **"GABONAISE"** alone, or **"GABON"** alone | modern, sporadic | Occurs on some modern designs and on much illegal/bogus material. **A bare "GABON" on a colourful modern-looking design is a red flag, not a period marker** — see §7. |

**Rule of thumb for a scanner:** `CONGO FRANÇAIS + GABON` → 1910;
`AFRIQUE EQUATORIALE + GABON` → c.1910–1933; `A.E.F.` with no Gabon → 1936–58, file under
French Equatorial Africa; `RÉPUBLIQUE GABONAISE` → 1959+.

## 6. Gaps — years with no Gabon-inscribed issues

1. **1891–1904 (the French Congo gap).** ~13 years. Gabon was administered as part of
   French Congo and used French Congo stamps. Catalogue country: **French Congo**.
2. **c. 1934/1936 – 1958 (the AEF gap) — the big one.** Sources disagree slightly on the
   opening edge and this must be recorded as a range, not a point:
   - Gabon's last own definitives are the **1932–1933** series.
   - Big Blue states Gabon's separate issues ended with administrative consolidation, with
     "1934" as the administrative date and **1936** as the year the first AEF-inscribed
     stamps appeared (overprints on Gabon and Middle Congo stock).
   - StampWorld and StampWorldHistory both bracket the AEF period as **1936–1958**.
   - **Recommended encoding: Gabon-inscribed issues cease after 1933; the AEF issuer runs
     1936–1958; 1934–1935 is a genuine dead zone with no new Gabon or AEF stamps
     identified.** Do not assert 1934 or 1935 issues without evidence.
3. **28 Nov 1958 – 27 Nov 1959.** The Republic existed but issued no stamps; AEF stamps
   remained in use. Exactly one year, closed by the 28 Nov 1959 M'ba pair.

Total span with no Gabon-inscribed stamps: roughly **1891–1904** and **1934–1959**, i.e.
about 39 of the 140 years since 1886.

## 7. Forgery risk and wallpaper risk

### Classic-era forgery risk: HIGH and specific

Named forgers and targets (from stampforgeries.com):

- **1886 handstamps "GAB 5", "GAB 25", "GAB 50", "GAB 75"** — **Fournier** forgeries.
  Described elsewhere as "extensively forged". Because the genuine article is a *handstamp
  on common stock*, the barrier to forgery is close to zero: the underlying French Colonies
  stamp is cheap and genuine, and only the ink mark is fake. **This is the single highest-risk
  item in Gabon philately.** Genuine examples start around $65 catalogue and go far higher,
  so the incentive is large. Expertisation is effectively mandatory.
- **1888–1889 surcharged 15 and 25** — forged surcharges.
- **1889 "Gabon-Congo / POSTES" 15 and 25** — **Fournier**.
- **1894 French Colonies Navigation & Commerce, values 1c, 2c, 15c, 20c, 35c, 1fr, 2fr, 5fr** —
  **Hirschburger**.
- **1912 Navigation & Commerce surcharged "10"** — **Hirschburger**.

Practical rule: **any overprinted or surcharged Gabon item before 1925 is guilty until proven
innocent.** The plain 1910 and 1932 pictorial definitives are comparatively safe.

### Wallpaper risk: the reputation is DESERVED — but only for a specific window, and it is
### mostly NOT Gabon's own doing

This required a real check, because two sources conflict:

- **StampWorldHistory states post-independence Gabon "has had a relatively modest issuing
  policy."** For the 1959–1990s period this appears correct: Gabon is not a classic
  agency-flooded country in the way Guinea, Central African Republic, or Guinea-Bissau are.
- **But the modern picture is dominated by outright illegal material, not by over-issuing.**
  The evidence is quantitative and damning. For **2018**, golowesstamps documents that
  **only 24 stamps were genuinely registered with the UPU** — a single Constitutional Court
  themed issue, dated 31 December 2018, printed by **Cartor** — while approximately
  **63+ counterfeit sets** appeared in Gabon's name in the same year. Illegal output
  outnumbered legitimate output by an order of magnitude. The same site documents a 2020
  set of Gabon illegal issues.
- **Named producer/distributor networks** for the fake Gabon material: Russian producers
  operating via *topnimarka.com*; a network branded *globalphila* distributing through eBay;
  and *spectre-stamps* trading under the aliases *sgistamps* and *arctic-stamps*.
- **UPU status:** the Worldwide Philatelic Modernisation Observatory (PWMO) list, as of its
  May 2016 revision, places **Gabon in the African and Arab Regions group of countries that
  "have witnessed the appearance of illegal stamps in their names" and are "in the process of
  organizing the relevant denouncements."** Critically, **Gabon has no published UPU circular
  number** — unlike Angola, Benin, and Cameroon which do. So: Gabon is a *named victim* of
  illegal issues, but **there is no formal UPU circular denouncing Gabon issues that I could
  verify.** Anyone citing "the UPU circular on Gabon" should be asked for the number.
- **IGPC:** IGPC represents 70+ countries and is widely criticised for excessive issues.
  **I could not verify that IGPC holds or has held the Gabon agency.** Do not assert it.
  The documented modern Gabon problem is *counterfeit* material from grey-market networks,
  which is a different and more serious category than agency over-issuing.

**Conclusion:** the "wallpaper" label is the wrong diagnosis. Gabon's problem from roughly
**2010 onward, intensifying sharply around 2018**, is **illegal/counterfeit issues in Gabon's
name**, not agency wallpaper. For a catalogue skeleton this matters: wallpaper is catalogued
(with disdain); illegal issues must be **excluded and flagged**. Any "Gabon" item dated
2018 outside the 24 Constitutional Court stamps is, per the reference cited, illegal.
CTO remainder material specifically: **not verified for Gabon — see §9.**

## 8. Image sourcing

- **Wikimedia Commons** has `Category:Stamps of Gabon` and `Category:Stamps of Gabon by year`,
  plus `Category:Stamps of French Congo` and `Category:Stamps of colonies of France`. Coverage
  is **thin** — the Gabon category was observed to be partly polluted with French Congo and
  German Cameroon material, and individual files noted include a 1984 Degas issue. Do not
  assume good per-issue coverage; verify item by item.
- **French copyright doctrine (this is the operative rule for the colonial material).**
  La Poste's own stated position is that French stamps have the same legal status as any
  other work of art: the design is protected for the **life of the designer plus 70 years**.
  Designer names are usually printed in the lower margin of the design. Commons applies this
  directly. A widely-repeated Commons benchmark is that **as of 1 January 2015 all French
  postage stamps issued up to 1922 were in the public domain** — that is a rolling
  approximation, not a bright line, and it depends on the individual artist's death date.
- **Practical consequence for Gabon.** The 1886–1922 material is the safest: the underlying
  Navigation and Commerce design is by **Louis-Eugène Mouchon (d. 1929)**, so that design
  entered the public domain in France around **2000**. The 1904–07 Gabon issue is therefore
  very likely reusable. The 1910 and 1932 pictorials require checking their individual
  designers' death dates and are **not** automatically clear.
- **Modern République Gabonaise issues (1959+) are the hard case.** Rights sit with the
  Gabonese post and/or the individual designers under Gabonese law, and Gabon is a Berne
  signatory. Assume **all rights reserved** unless a specific licence is found. Note that
  much modern "Gabon" imagery circulating online is of **illegal issues** (§7) — reproducing
  those carries a separate problem: the "rightsholder" is a counterfeiter, and the image
  misrepresents Gabon's philately.
- **Other avenues:** the Smithsonian National Postal Museum has a Gabon page under its
  Middle Africa exhibition — check its specific rights statement per object, as Smithsonian
  terms vary by collection and are not uniformly open. Dealer scans (Mystic, StampWorld,
  Colnect, LastDodo, HipStamp) are **not** licensed for reuse regardless of the underlying
  stamp's copyright status, because the photograph itself may carry a separate claim.
- **Recommended sourcing order:** Wikimedia Commons → own scans of physical material
  (pre-1922 designs) → institutional collections with explicit open licences → never
  dealer scrapes.

## 9. Uncertain — explicitly NOT verified

1. **A definitive Scott total for Gabon across all periods.** Only the classic-era figure
   (183 major numbers, 1886–1933, Scott Classic Specialized 2011) is solid. The modern
   Scott range is unverified.
2. **Stanley Gibbons and Michel totals for Gabon — no figure obtained at all.** The table in
   §1 has no SG or Michel row on purpose.
3. **Whether the ~1,775 Yvert figure is Y&T's own total or a Colnect artefact.** Colnect's
   Gabon/Y&T index was reported as 178 pages, which does not obviously reconcile with 1,775
   at Colnect's usual page size. Treat 1,775 as indicative only. StampWorld and Colnect both
   blocked automated fetch (HTTP 403 / anti-bot proof-of-work), so neither count was
   confirmed at source.
4. **The Scott 1–15 ↔ Yvert concordance for the 1886–1889 provisionals.** Not established.
5. **The exact printer of the 1904–07 Gabon Navigation and Commerce printing.** Attributed
   here to the Paris government stamp printing works on the strength of standard French
   colonial practice and the typographic method, **not on a source that names the printer
   for the Gabon values specifically.**
6. **The 1934–1935 dead zone.** Asserted from the absence of evidence, not from a source
   that positively states no stamps were issued. Big Blue's "1934" consolidation date versus
   the 1936 AEF start is an unresolved two-year seam.
7. **Free French / Vichy overprints.** The existence of the period is solid (AEF declared for
   Free France Aug 1940; Battle of Gabon Nov 1940), but **no source examined enumerated the
   actual "France Libre" / "Afrique Française Libre" overprints or their catalogue numbers.**
   The 1940–44 line in §3 is historical context, not verified philatelic listing.
8. **The Gabonese postal operator's legal name and its restructuring dates.** "OPT" and
   "La Poste Gabon / Poste Gabon S.A." are given from general knowledge and were **not
   confirmed by a source in this research.** Do not print them in a catalogue without checking.
9. **IGPC's involvement with Gabon** — neither confirmed nor refuted.
10. **CTO remainder material for Gabon** — no evidence found either way.
11. **Any UPU circular number for Gabon** — none exists as far as the PWMO May 2016 list shows.
    Post-2016 circulars were not checked; the UPU circulars page was identified but not read.
12. **The 28 Nov 1959 first-republic pair's catalogue numbers and printer.** Date and subject
    (two values, Prime Minister Léon M'ba) are corroborated; numbering is not.
13. **The 1965 Albert Schweitzer gold-foil issue**, claimed by Wikipedia as "one of the first
    gold foil stamps" — the *claim to primacy* is unverified and looks like exactly the kind of
    superlative that propagates without checking. Flagged, not endorsed.

### Additional myth flags

- **"Faidherbe type Gabon"** — false. Faidherbe is Senegal. See §2.
- **"Gabon's first stamps are the 1904 Navigation and Commerce"** — false, or at least
  imprecise. The first stamps are the 1886 handstamped provisionals; 1904 is the first
  *printed* Gabon issue.
- **"GAB" on pre-1886 covers means an overprint** — false. Before 1886 "GAB" is a
  **cancellation** applied to French Colonies general issues.
- **"Gabon is a wallpaper country"** — misleading. See §7: the modern problem is illegal
  counterfeit issues, not sanctioned over-issuing, and at least one country survey describes
  Gabon's own post-independence policy as *modest*.
- Dealer and aggregator pages (WorthPoint, MegaMinistore, and similar) returned in search
  results for Gabon show signs of generated boilerplate and were **not** used as primary
  evidence for any date or count in this dossier.
