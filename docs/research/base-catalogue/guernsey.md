# Guernsey
**Date:** 19 July 2026 · Base-catalogue dossier — Bailiwick of Guernsey (with Alderney and Sark
addressed as distinct catalogue questions, per the research brief).

## 1. Headline counts

| | |
|---|---|
| **Guernsey** (independent era, 1969–2026) — StampWorld count | **~2,109** |
| **Guernsey** (independent + pre-independence regional era) — StampData.com issuer node | **~1,975** |
| **Guernsey occupation issues (1940–44)** — Michel `Deutsche Besetzungsausgaben` count | **3 base designs** (~5–9 catalogue minors incl. shades/papers) |
| **Alderney** (1983–2026) — StampData.com issuer node, catalogued **separately** from Guernsey | **~821** |
| **Sark** (Aug 2025–2026) — first valid postage ever; too new for a stable catalogue count | **~12 stamps** (2 sets, per Linn's) |

⚠️ **No count here should be trusted to better than ±15–20%.** StampWorld's 2,109 and
StampData's 1,975 for "Guernsey" are close enough to be mutually corroborating, but they are
counting **different spans** — StampWorld's page is explicitly scoped "1969–2026" (independent
era only), while the StampData figure appears to fold in the 1958–1969 Great-Britain-regional
Guernsey issues as well. Neither figure was cross-checked against a physical Scott or SG volume;
treat 1,975–2,109 as "roughly two thousand **independent-era** Guernsey majors" and stop there.
**I trust StampData's inclusion logic slightly more** because it explicitly separates Alderney
into its own issuer ID (305) rather than folding it into Guernsey's, which matches how Scott and
SG both treat it (see below) — but I could not independently verify either site's number against
Scott or SG directly (both blocked automated fetches in earlier work on sibling dossiers in this
corpus, and the same access pattern was assumed here rather than re-tested stamp-by-stamp).

**Is Alderney counted inside Guernsey's total, or as its own catalogue country?**
**As its own catalogue country**, by both Scott (Volume 3, listed immediately after Guernsey,
within the Great Britain section of the catalogue) and Michel/Colnect (Alderney stamps carry
their own Colnect country ID, 261, distinct from Guernsey's 242/304). This is **not** a trivial
distinction: Alderney is politically **not** a separate jurisdiction — it is administered as
part of the Bailiwick of Guernsey and its stamps are issued *by* Guernsey Post — but philatelic
catalogues nonetheless give it a full independent country listing with its own number sequence.
**A schema keyed only on "Guernsey" will silently miss ~800 Alderney majors.** Practically: this
corpus should carry `Guernsey` and `Alderney` as two separate issuing entities linked by a
succession/subsidiary edge, exactly as it already does for other GB-administered island issuers.

**Sark: this is the single most important correction to the brief's assumption.** The brief
states Sark "has NO valid postal issues... locals/cinderellas" — **that was true until 27 August
2025.** On that date, under Guernsey Post's philatelic administration, Sark issued its **first
ever valid postage stamps**: an 8-stamp commemorative set (Occupation/Liberation themed, marking
the 80th anniversary of Sark's Liberation) on 27 Aug 2025, followed by a 15-value wildflower
definitive series on 3 Sept 2025 (Linn's Stamp News, guernseystamps.com — see Sources). Guernsey
Post's own material calls this "the first island to introduce [Bailiwick] stamps in more than 40
years, since Alderney's debut in 1983." **Everything Sark-related before Aug 2025 — including
the well-known 1960s–2020s "Sark" cinderellas/locals sold to tourists — remains non-postal and
should not be catalogued as valid postage.** But as of this writing Sark is a live, valid,
Guernsey-administered stamp-issuing entity, less than a year old, and any base-catalogue
treatment of Guernsey must add Sark as a **new** third sub-issuer (alongside Guernsey proper and
Alderney) rather than dismiss it as a locals-only case.

## 2. First issue

Two genuinely different "firsts" exist, and catalogues split on which is Guernsey No. 1:

- **1940–41 GB bisects (emergency, not a formal "issue")**: with occupation cutting off stamp
  supply, German authorities authorised (24 Dec 1940) the diagonal bisection of GB 2d stamps to
  produce two 1d values; first cancellations recorded **27 December 1940**. Five different GB 2d
  printings are known bisected. Used until **22 February 1941**, when the new locally-produced
  definitives took over. These are **provisional/emergency usages of Great Britain stamps**, not
  a Guernsey-inscribed issue — catalogues generally list them as a variety/usage note under the
  relevant GB stamp rather than assigning them their own Guernsey major number, though dealers
  frequently (and confusingly) describe them with ad hoc "Guernsey" numbers that do not
  correspond to any catalogue's actual listing. Treat any specific "Scott/SG number" claimed for
  a bisect as unverified until checked against a physical catalogue.
- **1941–44 Occupation Arms definitives**: designed by **E. W. (Ted) Vaudin**, first values issued
  **18–19 February 1941** (sources differ by a day). Design: **three heraldic lions / arms of
  Guernsey and the Duke of Normandy**, with four small "V for Victory" marks worked into the
  design as a quiet act of resistance — the same base design was shared with Jersey. Three
  denominations (½d, 1d, 2½d) form the core set, expanded 1941–44 with numerous shade and paper
  varieties; the 2½d value did not appear until 1944. Printer not confirmed in this research (see
  Uncertain). **Michel numbers this series 1–3** (with sub-letters for colour/paper varieties),
  but crucially **catalogues it under `Deutsche Besetzungsausgaben` (German WWII Occupation
  issues) → Kanalinseln (Channel Islands) section — i.e. under Germany, not under Guernsey's own
  country listing.** Scott and Stanley Gibbons, by contrast, give Guernsey occupation stamps their
  own listing under the **Guernsey** country heading (SG evidence found: "SG 2" dated 19 Feb 1941
  for the occupation series) — this is the key catalogue-heading split described in section 10.
- **1 October 1969 — first independent postal administration issue.** The States of Guernsey Post
  Office Board took over from the GPO/Royal Mail on this date. First definitive series (16 face
  values per one source citing "Scott 8/23"; **this specific Scott range is not independently
  confirmed and should be checked against a physical catalogue** — see Uncertain) designed by
  **Eric Piprell**, depicting the Queen's portrait off-centre, the **Crown of William the
  Conqueror**, and a stylised **Guernsey lily**; printed initially by **Harrison & Sons**
  (photogravure) for the low values with the four high values (2/6, 5/-, 10/-, £1) recess-printed
  by **Bradbury Wilkinson**. 70,000 first-day covers were prepared and ~160,000 stamps + 13,000
  covers sold on the first day (Guernsey Post's own 50th-anniversary retrospective).

**Which does each catalogue treat as "Guernsey No. 1"?** Evidence gathered here (dealer listings
citing "SG 2, 19/FEB/1941" for an occupation stamp, and a combined "Scott Guernsey & Alderney
album, 1941–2020") points to **both Scott and SG treating the 1941 occupation Arms definitive as
the start of Guernsey's own country listing (No. 1), with the 1969 independent series continuing
the same numbering sequence rather than restarting.** This is the opposite of how Jersey's
occupation issues are sometimes described (with an "N"-prefix implying a separate block), and I
could not fully reconcile the conflicting signals in the time available — **flagged as Uncertain
below.** Michel is unambiguous: its "Guernsey" country listing (Mi:GG prefix, seen at numbers in
the 1000s+ for modern issues) begins with the 1969 independent series; the 1941–44 stamps sit
entirely in Michel's Germany-occupation catalogue, not in "GG."

## 3. Issuing periods table

| Period | Dates | Issuing authority |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-occupation | to June 1940 | Great Britain / GPO (ordinary GB stamps used) |
| German bisects (emergency) | 24 Dec 1940 – 22 Feb 1941 | German occupation civil authorities, using bisected GB stock |
| German occupation definitives | 18/19 Feb 1941 – May 1945 (sold until ~13 Apr 1946) | German occupation civil authorities (design by E. W. Vaudin) |
| Liberation / GB regional (no distinct Guernsey stamps) | May 1945 – 17 Aug 1958 | Great Britain / GPO — ordinary GB definitives used |
| GB Regional issues for Guernsey | 18 Aug 1958 – 30 Sep 1969 | Great Britain / GPO (regional definitives, catalogued under Great Britain by SG; used specifically in Guernsey but valid UK-wide) |
| Independent Guernsey postal administration | 1 Oct 1969 – 2001 | States of Guernsey Post Office Board |
| Guernsey Post Ltd | 2001 – present | Guernsey Post Ltd (commercialised/licensed operator; Universal Service Obligation holder) |
| Alderney (own designated stamps within the Bailiwick) | 14 Jun 1983 – present | Guernsey Post Ltd, on Alderney's behalf, per a 1975–83 negotiated compromise |
| Sark (own designated stamps within the Bailiwick) | 27 Aug 2025 – present | Guernsey Post Ltd, on Sark's behalf — **newly established, first valid Sark postage ever** |

## 4. Predecessor / successor issuers (catalogue-naming terms)

- **Great Britain** — the predecessor for all Guernsey mail before 1940 and again for the whole
  1945–1969 gap (ordinary GB stamps), plus the specific **"Great Britain — Regional Issues,
  Guernsey"** sub-listing for 1958–1969 (catalogued under GB, not as a Guernsey country entry).
- **Germany / Deutsches Reich, Occupation issues (Kanalinseln)** — the catalogue heading Michel
  uses for the 1941–44 stamps; Scott/SG instead keep these under a **Guernsey** country heading.
- **Guernsey** (post-1969, States of Guernsey Post Office Board → Guernsey Post Ltd) — the
  primary modern issuer.
- **Alderney** — successor-by-delegation: Guernsey Post issues stamps *inscribed* Alderney from
  1983, catalogued as its own country by Scott/Michel/Colnect but administratively a Guernsey
  Post product.
- **Sark** — same relationship as Alderney, newly created Aug 2025; too recent to know how
  catalogues will format its country heading (as of this writing, expect it to follow the
  Alderney precedent — its own listing, administered by Guernsey Post).

## 5. Inscriptions (text actually printed on the stamps)

- **1941–44 occupation issues:** no country name at all in the usual sense — the design is the
  Guernsey/Normandy arms; occupation-era Guernsey stamps are typically identified by design and
  postmark rather than an inscribed place name (this research could not confirm an explicit
  printed word "GUERNSEY" on the occupation stamps themselves — flagged as Uncertain).
- **1958–1969 GB Regional issues:** inscribed simply with the monarch's cipher/portrait per the
  standard British regional pattern; "GUERNSEY" does not necessarily appear as printed text —
  regionals are identified by the design (Guernsey lily / Crown of William the Conqueror), not by
  a country name printed on the stamp face. **Not independently confirmed here — verify.**
- **1969–present independent Guernsey:** inscribed **`GUERNSEY`** and, on many issues,
  **`BAILIWICK OF GUERNSEY`**.
- **Alderney issues (1983–present):** carry a **dual inscription** — typically **`ALDERNEY`**
  paired with **`BAILIWICK OF GUERNSEY`** — the key identification point distinguishing an
  Alderney-inscribed stamp from a plain Guernsey one while confirming the shared postal
  administration. This dual wording is exactly the kind of detail a search/schema needs to
  capture as an explicit inscription-alias pair.
- **Sark issues (2025–present):** presumed to follow the same dual-inscription pattern
  (`SARK` / `BAILIWICK OF GUERNSEY`) by analogy with Alderney, but this was **not directly
  confirmed** against an image of the actual 27 Aug / 3 Sep 2025 stamps in this research —
  flagged as Uncertain.

## 6. Gaps

- **1945 – 17 August 1958 (~13 years):** no Guernsey-specific stamps at all; ordinary Great
  Britain definitives were used throughout the Bailiwick after Liberation (9 May 1945) until the
  first GB Regional issue for Guernsey appeared in August 1958. (Guernsey Post's own account
  mentions a 1947 Liberation-anniversary GB commemorative pair, which is a UK-wide issue, not
  Guernsey-specific.)
- **1958 – 1 October 1969 (~11 years):** Guernsey had GB Regional stamps (its own design, but
  catalogued under Great Britain, valid UK-wide) rather than a fully independent country listing
  — a genuine "hides under a different country heading" trap, not a true issuing gap, but one
  that a Guernsey-keyed search could easily miss.
- **1969–present:** no gap identified; Guernsey Post has issued regularly and, per its own
  releases, 10–16 stamp-issue events per year through 2023–2025.

## 7. Forgery risk / wallpaper risk

**Forgery risk — real and specific, concentrated in the occupation era:**
- The **1940 GB bisects are heavily faked, especially on cover.** Only ~600 of the 1924-design
  2d GB stamps are recorded as genuinely bisected during the authorised window (24 Dec 1940 – 22
  Feb 1941); "faked bisect and forged postmark" combinations are explicitly documented by
  forgery-reference sources (stampforgeries.com), and expert certification (BPP/RPSL-level
  expertise) is standard practice before high-value bisect covers change hands.
- The **1941–44 occupation Arms definitives (Michel 1–3)** are also known-forged — dedicated
  forgery reference pages (stampforgeries.com) give side-by-side genuine-vs-forged comparisons
  for all three base denominations, and recommend consulting BPP-affiliated experts (e.g.
  Michael Wieneke) for authentication.
- No evidence found of systematic forgery risk for the post-1969 independent-era Guernsey,
  Alderney, or (obviously, given its age) Sark issues — modern low-value commemoratives of small
  philatelic-bureau territories are not a typical forgery target; the risk profile is
  concentrated entirely in the scarce, high-value 1940s occupation material.

**Wallpaper risk — Guernsey is a borderline case, and the honest answer is "not clearly, but
watch it":**
- Guernsey Post issued **16 stamp-release events in 2023, 10 in 2024, and 14 in 2025** by its
  own count — each event typically a set of several stamps plus souvenir sheets — which for a
  combined Guernsey+Alderney(+now Sark) population of **~65,000–68,000** is a meaningfully high
  per-capita issue rate. Alderney runs a broadly similar release cadence in parallel.
- However, **Guernsey Post is the issuing authority itself** (via its own Philatelic Bureau,
  founded on the same footing as any national postal operator), not an offshore agency mill —
  this is structurally different from IGPC/Stamperija clients where a private company holds the
  contract and drives volume for pure collector-market extraction. Guernsey's printers include
  reputable, non-wallpaper-associated security printers (**Cartor**, **BDT International
  Security Printers**), and its releases are marketed through **WOPA+** (World Online Philatelic
  Agency), a legitimate multi-territory online storefront selling at official face value — a
  different model from Stamperija's direct-to-collector wallpaper catalogue, though still an
  agency-distribution channel worth noting.
- **No evidence found that Guernsey, Alderney, or Sark have ever appeared on a UPU illegal-issue
  circular.** The UPU's illegal-issue mechanism targets stamps produced *without* the named
  territory's authorisation; Guernsey Post issues under its own name with its own authority, so
  it does not fit that category by definition.
- **Verdict: Guernsey (with Alderney) runs a busy, collector-oriented, agency-marketed issuing
  programme that a strict "genuine postal necessity" test would criticise as excessive for the
  population — but it is not an illegitimate or IGPC/Stamperija-style wallpaper operation.** It
  sits closer to San Marino/Faroe-Islands-style small-territory philatelic marketing than to the
  Sahara-Occidental/Nevis school of illegal fantasy issues. Flag it for **moderate** wallpaper
  caution in the schema (issue-rate metadata worth capturing), not exclusion.

**Cinderella/locals pollution — confirmed and should be filtered, with one now-partial
exception:**
- **Herm** issued its own local stamps **1949–1969** (no official GPO post office on Herm;
  stamps covered the cost of ferrying mail to Guernsey, alongside a UK stamp for onward postage)
  — a genuine local-function issue but still a **cinderella**, never valid as UK/Guernsey
  postage in its own right.
- **Jethou** issued local stamps **1960–1969**, same category.
- **Sark** issued tourist "local" cinderella labels for decades before 2025 — **these pre-2025
  Sark items remain cinderellas and must not be conflated with the genuine Aug/Sep 2025 Guernsey
  Post-issued Sark stamps** (see section 1). This is now a live disambiguation problem: "Sark"
  search results will contain both valid postage (2025+) and non-postal locals (pre-2025), and a
  schema needs a hard date-based or issuer-based split, not a blanket "Sark = cinderella" rule.
- **Lundy** is a Bristol Channel island unrelated to the Bailiwick of Guernsey (it is off North
  Devon) but is frequently offered alongside Channel Islands locals in dealer listings under a
  loose "GB Offshore Islands" grouping — a search/data-hygiene trap worth noting even though
  Lundy has no Guernsey connection.
- General rule for this corpus: any "island local" inscribed with a Channel Islands place name
  that is *not* Guernsey, Alderney, or (post-Aug-2025) Sark issued by Guernsey Post, is a
  cinderella and should be tagged as such, not merged into the Guernsey issuer record.

## 8. Image sourcing

- **Guernsey Post Ltd is a going commercial entity and actively asserts copyright** over its
  stamp designs; its own retail site (guernseystamps.com) is the "ask permission first" channel
  the general research consulted for this project's sibling dossiers has repeatedly flagged for
  Crown-copyright-style postal administrations. No blanket public-domain or Crown-copyright
  carve-out for Guernsey Post's own designs was found in this research — **assume post-1969
  Guernsey, Alderney, and Sark stamp designs are in copyright, held by Guernsey Post Ltd (or its
  designers/printers under contract), and require a licence for reuse**, consistent with
  standard UK/Commonwealth postal-administration practice.
- **The 1941–44 German occupation issues** are a genuinely different case: designed under wartime
  occupation authority (E. W. Vaudin), nearly 85 years old, and the original issuing authority
  (German occupation administration) no longer exists in any successor form that would hold
  rights. This raises a real possibility that the occupation-era designs are functionally
  public-domain or at minimum far lower-risk than the modern Guernsey Post catalogue — **but this
  was not confirmed against an actual copyright-term analysis (UK Crown Copyright rules do not
  obviously apply to a German wartime civil-occupation design)** and should be treated as
  Uncertain rather than assumed safe.
- **The 1958–1969 GB Regional issues** are UK Crown Copyright material (Royal Mail/GPO-era
  designs); UK Crown Copyright artistic works generally run 50 years from creation for this era,
  which would place most of these regionals into the public domain already — but this needs
  verification against the specific UK Crown Copyright term rules in force for the relevant
  years, not assumed from general principle.
- **Wikimedia Commons** has a `Category:Postage stamps of Guernsey` (confirmed to exist as a
  subcategory of `Category:Stamps of the United Kingdom`); this research did not get an exact
  image count for that category and it should be measured directly (per this project's standard
  `corpus-measurements.md` methodology) rather than assumed. Given Guernsey's ~2,000-stamp
  independent-era catalogue, Commons coverage is very likely a small fraction, consistent with
  every other territory measured so far in this corpus.
- **Practical sourcing path:** reusable images for the occupation era are more likely to
  originate from historical/philatelic-literature scans (RPSL, Channel Islands specialist
  societies) than from Commons; reusable images for the modern era are effectively gated by
  Guernsey Post's own copyright and are the least likely of any period here to be freely
  licensable at scale.

## 9. Uncertain — flagged, not asserted

- **Whether Scott/SG number the 1941 occupation issue as continuous with the 1969 independent
  series, or as a separate "N"-prefixed block** (as Jersey's occupation issues are sometimes
  described). Evidence gathered pointed both ways and could not be reconciled against a physical
  catalogue in this research — this is the single most important open question for assigning any
  StampID scheme to early Guernsey material, and should be resolved against a physical Scott/SG
  volume before any numbering decision is encoded.
- **The precise Scott/SG catalogue numbers for the 1940 bisects, the 1941–44 definitives, and the
  1 Oct 1969 first series** — a dealer listing citing "Scott 8/23" for the 1969 series and
  another citing "SG14b = Scott 28" for a booklet pane were the only numeric anchors found; none
  were cross-checked against a primary catalogue. Do not encode any of these numbers as
  authoritative without independent verification.
- **The printer of the 1941–44 occupation definitives** — not established in this research.
- **Whether the occupation stamps carry any printed "GUERNSEY" text** or are identified purely by
  design/postmark — not confirmed.
- **Exact inscription wording on the 2025 Sark stamps** — assumed by analogy with Alderney's dual
  inscription, not confirmed against an image.
- **StampWorld's 2,109 and StampData's 1,975 counts** — neither was reconciled against Scott,
  Michel, or Colnect directly; Colnect's own total count for Guernsey was not obtainable via
  search (Colnect's site structure returns per-year/per-series pages rather than a single total).
- **Alderney's 821-stamp StampData count** — same caveat; not cross-checked against Scott/SG/Michel.
- **Guernsey Post Ltd's precise legal status** (2001 change) — described here as
  "commercialisation" rather than full privatisation based on limited source material; the exact
  legal/ownership structure (States of Guernsey ownership stake, if any) was not fully verified.
