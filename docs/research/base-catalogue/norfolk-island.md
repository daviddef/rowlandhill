# Norfolk Island

Research dossier for base-catalogue seeding. Compiled 2026-07-19. Every figure below is tagged
with its source; anything not directly verified is marked **Uncertain**.

---

## 1. Headline counts

Norfolk Island is a **closed issuer**. It issued its own stamps from **10 June 1947** to
**7 June 2016**, a span of 69 years. From 1 July 2016 the Australian postal system took over and
"Norfolk Island" survives only as an inscription on Australia Post issues — not as a separate
catalogue country.

**Which figure to trust:** for a *count of distinct collectable major stamps*, use **~1,200 (Colnect,
1947–2016)**. It is the only source that publishes a transparent, auditable year-by-year breakdown
that can be re-summed. Treat StampData's 1,298 as the upper bound (post-2016 inclusive). Do **not**
quote an SG or Scott terminal number without opening the physical catalogue — none of the online
sources checked state one, and dealer pages that assert one are unverifiable.

**Closure year: 2016.** Final stamp-issuing year and final year of independent postal operation.

## 2. First issue

| Field | Value | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| Date | **10 June 1947** | **High** — Wikipedia, Pacific Union College (PISC) LibGuide and dealer/catalogue listings all agree on 10 June 1947. |
| Catalogue numbers | **SG 1–12** | **High** on SG 1 = ½d Ball Bay and on the set being 12 stamps ½d–2s. The clean 1–12 mapping is a reasonable inference; SG 12 is confirmed in dealer listings as a Ball Bay 1947 value. |
| Set size / values | 12 stamps, **½d to 2s** | High |
| Subject | **Ball Bay** — a single view of Ball Bay used for all twelve values, with the inscription "FOUNDED 1788" (the first penal settlement) | High |
| Designer/engraver | **Frank Manley** | Medium-high (Wikipedia; "Manley's Ball Bay design") |
| Printer | **Note Printing Branch, Commonwealth Bank of Australia, Melbourne** | **Verified** — the exact wording appears in both the PISC LibGuide and Wikipedia-derived sources. The task's tentative attribution is correct. |
| Perforation | 14 per 2 cm | Medium |

**Notable pre-history / myth check:** a Ball Bay printing was prepared in **1940** but never issued
because of wartime conditions; the stock was destroyed in 1947, but some examples were **stolen and
reached the philatelic market**. The 1940 material is distinguishable by perforation from the 1947
issue. This is a genuine, documented rarity — but it means dealer listings offering "1940 Norfolk
Island" stamps are describing stolen unissued material, not a first issue. **Norfolk Island's first
issue is 1947, not 1940.**

## 3. Issuing periods

| Era | Dates | Issuing authority | Stamps used / issued |
|---|---|---|---|
| Van Diemen's Land period | **1854–1855** (Uncertain, see §4) | Van Diemen's Land (Tasmania) colonial post | Van Diemen's Land stamps |
| Abandonment / Pitcairner arrival | 1855 – 1877 | none / no regular service | No regular postal service. Mail ad hoc via shipping. |
| New South Wales period | **1877–1913** | New South Wales colonial post (irregular service from 1877; regular service from 1897) | New South Wales stamps |
| Commonwealth of Australia period | **1913–1947** | Australian Postmaster-General's Department | Australian (Commonwealth) stamps |
| **Independent issuer** | **10 June 1947 – 7 June 2016** | Norfolk Island Administration / **Norfolk Island Postal Service (Norfolk Island Post)** | Own stamps, inscribed NORFOLK ISLAND. Valid **only on Norfolk Island**; Australian stamps were not valid on the island and Norfolk stamps were not valid in Australia. |
| **Australian postal system** | **from 1 July 2016** | **Australia Post** | Australia Post issues inscribed "Norfolk Island / Australia", valid throughout Australia and its territories — same arrangement as Christmas Island and Cocos (Keeling) Islands. |

Note the key structural fact: Norfolk Island was the **last Australian external territory to remain
postally independent**, after Christmas Island and Cocos (Keeling) had already been absorbed into
Australia Post.

## 4. Predecessor / successor issuers

**Predecessors (exact catalogue names):**

1. **Van Diemen's Land** (SG/Scott country "Tasmania"; earliest stamps 1853) — reported in use
   1854–1855, when the second penal settlement was administered from Hobart.
   **⚠ FLAG — verify before seeding.** This claim traces to a single Wikipedia line that is then
   echoed by StampWorldHistory and dealer pages. The second settlement was wound down and the island
   evacuated by **May 1855**, giving a window of barely a year. No source checked cites a surviving
   cover. Treat "Van Diemen's Land stamps used on Norfolk Island" as **plausible but not
   independently confirmed**, and beware AI-generated dealer text that states it as settled fact.
2. **New South Wales** — 1877–1913. Irregular service 1877, regular service 1897. Well attested.
3. **Australia** (Commonwealth of Australia) — **1913–1947**. This is the immediate predecessor and
   is the one to record as the direct precursor issuer.

**Successor:**

- **Australia** / **Australia Post**. Norfolk Island Post ceased providing postal services on
  **1 July 2016** under the Norfolk Island reforms that ended the island's self-government.
- **Last Norfolk Island stamp: 7 June 2016** — a **$5 value in a souvenir sheet** marking the 160th
  anniversary of the landing of the Pitcairners (the landing itself was 8 June 1856).
- Australia Post's **first** "Norfolk Island, Australia" issue was the **Sea Birds** set,
  **20 September 2016**, depicting the red-tailed tropicbird and the masked booby.

So: last independent issue **7 June 2016**; administrative cutoff **1 July 2016**; first successor
issue **20 September 2016**. These are three different dates and are frequently conflated.

## 5. Inscriptions

Printed on the stamps themselves:

- **`NORFOLK ISLAND`** — the constant inscription across the entire 1947–2016 independent period.
- **`FOUNDED 1788`** — additionally on the 1947 Ball Bay definitives.
- **`NORFOLK ISLAND` + `AUSTRALIA` together** — the identification field of interest.
  - **First co-occurrence: 1959.** Norfolk Island took the Australian GPO's 1959 design marking 150
    years of the Post Office in Australia and issued it overprinted `NORFOLK ISLAND` (in red) with
    the value revalued from 4d to 5d — so the original `AUSTRALIA` remains on the stamp alongside
    the new `NORFOLK ISLAND`. Two independent sources (PISC LibGuide; secondary summaries) agree.
    **Medium-high confidence.**
  - **⚠ MYTH FLAG:** the widely repeated claim that `AUSTRALIA` never appeared on a Norfolk Island
    stamp until Australia Post took over in 2016 is **wrong** — the 1959 overprint is the
    counter-example. For OCR/identification purposes, presence of `AUSTRALIA` alone does **not**
    date a stamp to post-2016.
  - **Systematic co-inscription: from 20 September 2016**, all Australia Post issues carry
    "Norfolk Island" together with "Australia" as a matter of course.

## 6. Gaps

- **1940–1946**: no issued stamps. A Ball Bay printing was produced in 1940 but withheld because of
  the war and destroyed in 1947 (some stolen — see §2). Australian stamps were in use throughout.
- **1948–1952**: no new issues between the 1947 definitives and the second definitive series of
  **10 June 1953**.
- **1954–1955, 1957**: no issues per Colnect's year list (first commemorative was 8 June 1956).
- **2009 and 2012**: **no issues** per Colnect's year-by-year table (it skips 2009 and 2012 entirely).
  This falls inside the 2008–2013 collapse in output — see §7.
- Post-**2016**: no further Norfolk Island issues as a separate issuer, permanently.

## 7. Forgery risk and wallpaper risk

**Forgery risk: LOW.** Norfolk Island has no classic high-value rarities of the kind that attract
counterfeiters. The 1947 Ball Bay set is common and inexpensive. The one genuine authentication
question is the **1940 unissued Ball Bay printing** (stolen stock), which is distinguished from the
1947 issue by perforation — that is a niche specialist problem, not a mass-forgery problem. Modern
material is too cheap to forge. Expect the real-world risk to be **misattribution** (Norfolk vs.
Australia vs. other Pacific issuers) rather than fakes.

**Wallpaper risk: MODERATE — and Norfolk Island is NOT an agency wallpaper issuer.** Say this
plainly: Norfolk Island was **not** a Nordfrim/IGPC/Stamperija-style agency country. Its stamps were
issued by a real postal administration for a real, if tiny, resident population, and they were
genuinely valid for postage on the island. It has never appeared on the UPU/WADP illegal-issue lists
and is a fully accepted catalogue country in Scott, SG and Michel.

**However — flag the boom period.** Output was clearly philatelically driven, aimed at thematic
collectors, and became disproportionate to a population of ~1,700:

- 1947–1959: 0–12 stamps/year.
- 1980s: 17–39/year.
- **1998–2007: the peak — 31 to 49 stamps per year, ten consecutive years above 30.** 2001 (49),
  2003 (48), 2004 (47), 2002 (46) are the high-water marks.
- **2008–2012: collapse** — 4, 0, 3, 1, 0. This coincides with Norfolk Island's financial crisis.
- 2013–2015: brief revival (20, 38, 34), then closure.

Roughly **one third of the entire 69-year output falls in the single decade 1998–2007.** For
valuation purposes, treat that decade as low-scarcity thematic material.

## 8. Image sourcing

- **Wikimedia Commons: essentially no Norfolk Island stamp images.** Commons' *Stamps/Oceania* page
  gives Norfolk Island no dedicated entry and simply defers to *Copyright rules by
  territory/Australia § Stamps*. Do not plan on Commons as a source for this issuer.
- **Copyright status.** Norfolk Island stamps are governed by **Australian** copyright rules.
  Australia Post asserts copyright for **50 years from publication** on stamp designs (this is the
  short Crown-copyright-style term, *not* the life+70 term applied to ordinary Australian works).
- **Practical public-domain line.** Commons accepts Australian stamps published **before 1 January
  1976** under `{{PD-Australia}}`. On a rolling 50-year basis at the 2026 date of writing, the safe
  set is roughly **1947 through the mid-1970s** — which covers the Ball Bay definitives, the 1953
  second definitives, the 1956 first commemorative and the 1959 overprint.
- **Everything from ~1976 to 2016 is still in copyright** and cannot be freely reused. Options for
  that range: licensed catalogue imagery (Colnect/StampWorld are *not* reusable-licensed — their
  images are user-uploaded under restrictive terms), a direct licence from Australia Post, or
  first-party photography of physical stamps (photographing a copyrighted design does not clear the
  underlying copyright — flagged as a real constraint, not a workaround).
- **Uncertain:** whether pre-2016 Norfolk Island Administration designs vest in Australia Post at
  all, or in the (now-abolished) Norfolk Island Administration / the Commonwealth. The chain of
  title after the 2016 reforms was **not established** by any source checked. Get legal input before
  relying on the 50-year rule for Norfolk-specific designs.

2. **Terminal Scott number**, and Scott numbers generally. Not verified.
3. **Michel count and number range.** Not verified.
4. **StampWorld count.** Site blocks automated fetch (HTTP 403).
5. **Seven Seas numbering scheme and count.** Known to exist; content unverified. This is the
   catalogue Australian dealers actually quote, so it is the highest-value gap to close.
6. **ACSC coverage** for Norfolk Island. Source unreachable (connection reset).
7. **Whether SG 1–12 maps exactly to the twelve 1947 values.** SG 1 (½d) and SG 11/12 are confirmed
   in dealer listings; the intermediate mapping is inferred.
8. **Van Diemen's Land usage 1854–1855.** Single-sourced, short window, no cover evidence seen.
9. **Exact date the 1959 overprint was issued**, and whether any *earlier* Norfolk stamp carried
   "AUSTRALIA".

11. **Copyright chain of title** for pre-2016 Norfolk Island designs post-abolition.
12. **Exact count of post-2016 Australia Post "Norfolk Island" issues** (Wikipedia says ~20+ through
    2021; not carried further).

catalogue (11th/12th ed.), Scott Standard Postage Stamp Catalogue Vol. 5, Seven Seas *Australasian*
catalogue, ACSC Norfolk Island section.
