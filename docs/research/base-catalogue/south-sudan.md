# South Sudan — Base Catalogue Dossier
**Date:** 19 July 2026 · **Purpose:** establish what is actually catalogued for the world's
newest stamp-issuing country, and separate genuine postal issues from the noise around them.

---

| Measure | Count | Catalogue / source |
|---|---|---|
| Postage stamps listed 2011–2025 | **56** | **StampWorld** (its own numbering) |
| Stamps listed | **~35** | **Colnect** (search-result claim; page itself is bot-blocked — treat as soft) |

| Stanley Gibbons majors | **unknown — possibly none** | see below |
| Michel | listed in **Übersee ÜK 4/1 Nordafrika** | Michel volume scope (numbers not seen) |
| Wikimedia Commons reusable images | **0** | `Category:Stamps of South Sudan` returns **404** |

⚠️ **A count without a catalogue name is worthless here, and the spread is worse than the usual
±20%.** StampWorld's 56 and Colnect's ~35 differ by ~40%. Neither is a printed catalogue.
**Trust order: Scott > Michel > StampWorld > Colnect.** Scott is the only one confirmed by an
independent editorial source (Linn's) to be actively *reviewing* South Sudan rather than merely
carrying rows in a database. StampWorld's 56 almost certainly folds in the 2017 surcharge
sub-types and the 2024–25 material whose status is unresolved (§7).

⚠️ **Stanley Gibbons: unresolved, and the evidence leans negative.** The SG *North East Africa*
catalogue (1st ed. 2013, 360pp) advertises its scope as **Burundi, Djibouti, Egypt, Eritrea,
Ethiopia, Rwanda, Somalia and Sudan** — **South Sudan is not in the published scope list**, in an
edition printed two years *after* independence. That is a signal, not proof: SG may list it under
Sudan, or in a later edition. **Do not assert an SG number for any South Sudan stamp** until a
physical catalogue is checked. This is the single most important open item in this dossier.

## 2. First issue

| Field | Value | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| Date | **13 July 2011** | high (Wikipedia, Smithsonian NPM, Linn's all agree) |
| Independence | 9 July 2011 | high |
| UPU membership | **4 October 2011** | high |
| Printer | **Beijing Security Printers**, PRC | high |
| Provenance | a **gift from the People's Republic of China** | high |
| Issued values | **1 SSP** Flag, **3.50 SSP** Dr John Garang | high — **Scott 1–2** |
| Prepared but **withdrawn** | **2.50 SSP** Coat of Arms | high |
| Currency | South Sudanese Pound (SSP) | high |

⚠️ **Myth: "South Sudan's first stamps appeared on independence day, 9 July 2011."** They did
not. The set was *prepared* for 9 July; issue was **13 July**.

⚠️ **Myth: "the first issue is a set of three."** Only **two** stamps were validly issued. The
2.50 SSP Coat of Arms was pulled because the arms are **wrong** — the eagle faces right instead
of left, and carries **two spears** instead of one spear and one spade. Market copies of the
2.50 come from (a) commemorative covers and (b) singles reportedly sent by Beijing Security
Printers to its own clients as samples. **It is a withdrawn/unissued stamp, not a rarity of a
normal issue**, and anything offered as "postally used" on it deserves scepticism.

**Were the issued two genuinely sold over the counter?** Yes — the balance of evidence says the
1 SSP and 3.50 SSP were real post-office stock (they were still in the drawer in 2016, which is
why they were the stock overprinted in 2017). But collectors on the forums note the near-total
absence of genuine **non-philatelic** commercial covers, which is explained by §6 rather than by
fakery: after April 2012 there was effectively no outbound international mail.

## 3. Issuing periods

| Period | Issuing authority (catalogue-facing name) | Stamps of its own? |
|---|---|---|
| pre-1897 | **Egypt** (Egyptian stamps used in the Sudan) | no — Egypt's stamps |
| 1897 – 1955 | **Anglo-Egyptian Sudan** (Anglo-Egyptian Condominium, from 1899) | yes — from 1 Mar 1897 |
| 1 Jan 1956 – 8 Jul 2011 | **Republic of the Sudan** (Sudan; UPU 27 Jul 1956) | yes |
| 1972–1983 and 2005–2011 | **Southern Sudan** autonomous region / **Government of Southern Sudan** (GoSS) | **NO — see §4** |
| 9 Jul 2011 – present | **Republic of South Sudan** — Ministry of Telecommunications & Postal Services, then **South Sudan Post** | yes, from 13 Jul 2011 |

## 4. Predecessor / successor issuers

Chain a catalogue would actually walk:

```
Egypt ──1897──▶ Anglo-Egyptian Sudan ──1956──▶ Sudan ──9 Jul 2011──▶ South Sudan
                                                    └──────────────▶ Sudan (continues)
```

- **There are no separate pre-2011 South Sudan catalogue numbers.** All South Sudanese territory
  was catalogued under **Sudan** (and before 1956, **Anglo-Egyptian Sudan**; before 1897,
  **Egypt**). A search for "South Sudan" that does not traverse the succession edge to Sudan
  returns nothing before July 2011 — and that is 114 years of the territory's postal history.
- ⚠️ **The autonomous Southern Region (1972–83) and the Government of Southern Sudan (2005–11)
  never issued stamps.** Both were real polities with real administrations; neither is a
  catalogue issuer. Any label inscribed "Government of Southern Sudan" or "Southern Sudan"
  purporting to predate July 2011 has **no catalogue standing** and should be treated as
  fantasy material until someone produces a listing for it.
- **Sudan is the successor as well as the predecessor** — it continues issuing. This is a split,
  not a replacement, and the edge must be modelled as such.

## 5. Inscriptions

**South Sudan (2011– ):**

| Element | Printed text | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Country | `REPUBLIC OF SOUTH SUDAN` | English |
| Currency | `SSP` / South Sudanese Pound; values on the first two issues run 1 / 2.50 / 3.50, then 1–50 SSP; post-2020 values in the hundreds (50–1000 SSP) — inflation, not a new currency | |
| Arabic | **not established — see Uncertain** | |

South Sudan's **sole official language is English**, a deliberate constitutional break from
Sudan's Arabic. That makes an English-only inscription the *expected* pattern and a strong
identification signal — **but I could not find a source that explicitly states no Arabic appears
on the stamps**, and I did not read high-resolution images. Treat "English only" as the working
hypothesis, flagged, not as a fact.

**Predecessor inscriptions (needed to identify pre-2011 material):**

- **1897 first issue:** Egyptian stamps overprinted **`SOUDAN`** — in **French *and* Arabic**.
- **1898–1948 "Camel Postman" / "Desert Postman"** definitives, De La Rue — the long-running
  Sudan design; `SUDAN` / `SOUDAN` with Arabic script.
- **Republic of the Sudan (1956– ):** `SUDAN` with Arabic.
- The key discriminator for a scanner: **Arabic script present ⇒ almost certainly Sudan, not
  South Sudan.**

## 6. Gaps

South Sudan's programme is sparse and interrupted, and the gaps are real events, not
record-keeping failures.

| Years | Status | Cause |
|---|---|---|
| 2011 | 2 stamps issued (of 3 prepared) | — |
| 2012 (Apr) | **international mail connection ceases** | Sudan discontinued Juba–Khartoum direct flights; routing via Sudan ended. Some inbound mail only, via Ethiopia and later Kenya |
| 2012 | **an entire commemorative set prepared and proof-printed for President Salva Kiir's April 2012 China visit was never issued** | Heglig/border conflict; stock reportedly handed to the President's entourage and stored at the Office of the President in Juba. Sets have nonetheless surfaced on internet auction sites |
| 2012–2013 | 2nd definitive set: 1, 2, 5, 10, 20, 50 SSP — birds & wildlife (Shoebill, Bearded Vulture, Saddle-billed Stork, Nile Lechwe, Eland, African Fish Eagle). Delayed past its planned 9 Jul 2012 date. **20 SSP is captioned "White-eared Kob" but depicts an Eland** | |
| 2014 – 2016 | **no issues** | civil war from Dec 2013; postal service degraded |
| 15 Sep 2017 | surcharges overprinted on 2011 and 2012/13 stock, printed in **Uganda** | hyperinflation of the SSP; low values withdrawn late 2016 |
| 2018 – 2019 | **no issues** | |
| 21 Jul 2020 | COVID-19 "Tribute to healthcare personnel" — 6 values (50–1000 SSP) + miniature sheet (MS reportedly only **300** copies). A **joint African issue**; South Sudan was the only participant to alter the common design. Designed by the South Sudan Stamp Committee under Stanislaus Tombe Felix. **The first issue since 2017.** | |
| 2021 – 2023 | **no issues confirmed** | |
| 2024 – 2025 | material reported: 13th Anniversary of Independence (9 Jul 2024), **Mahatma Gandhi (3 Jun 2024)**, Sporting Associations, definitives (16 Jan 2025), UPU 150 | ⚠️ **status unverified — see §7** |

## 7. Forgery risk and wallpaper risk

### Is South Sudan a wallpaper issuer?

**On the evidence through 2020: no — and the reason matters.** South Sudan Post **had no
agreement with any international stamp agency for the marketing of its stamps**. That single
fact is the difference between South Sudan and the Stamperija/IGPC client states: there was no
agency, so there was no agency programme. The output is tiny (two stamps in 2011, six in
2012/13, surcharges in 2017, seven items in 2020), the subjects are entirely national (flag,
arms, Garang, indigenous birds and antelope), and the 2020 issue came out of a domestic stamp
committee with a named local designer.

⚠️ **But the 2024–2025 reports do not fit that pattern and I could not verify them.** A
**Mahatma Gandhi** commemorative from South Sudan is exactly the shape of agency-produced
topical filler, and a country that issued nothing 2021–2023 suddenly producing four programmes
in two years is the classic signature of an agency contract having been signed — or of someone
producing labels in its name. **Do not ingest 2024–2025 South Sudan issues as genuine without
independent confirmation.** They are the highest-risk records in the country.

### Illegal issues — what is actually established

- **South Sudan appears on the illegal-stamps watch list.** It is named among countries in the
  African and Arab Regions that "have witnessed the appearance of illegal stamps in their names"
  and are **in the process of organising denouncements to be published as UPU Circulars**.
- ⚠️ **No UPU circular denouncing South Sudan illegal issues was found.** The denouncement is
  described as *in process* — a state it appears to have been in for years. **Do not cite a UPU
  circular number for South Sudan; there does not appear to be one.** By contrast **Sudan** does
  have one: **UPU Circular No. 095/2004**.
- **WNS registration: not confirmed either way.** The WNS database (124,354 stamps) could not be
  filtered for South Sudan by automated fetch. **Absence of a WNS number for a South Sudan stamp
  proves nothing** on its own — many small administrations never joined WNS — but a *present*
  WNS number would be the single strongest legitimacy signal available. Worth a manual check.

### Specific contamination to encode

1. **The withdrawn 2.50 SSP Coat of Arms (2011).** Never validly issued. Circulates via covers
   and printer's samples. High premium ⇒ high forgery incentive.
2. **The unissued 2012 China-visit commemorative set.** Proof-printed, never distributed,
   **sold on internet auction sites anyway**. Anything from this set that is offered as an
   issued stamp is misdescribed by definition.
3. **2017 surcharge printer's waste.** The Ugandan printer produced "an endless stream" of
   mis-printed overprints which were **never shipped back to South Sudan Post for sale or
   destruction**. This is the most dangerous category in South Sudan philately: genuine paper,
   genuine overprint, genuine printer — but never postal stock, and now indistinguishable-looking
   in a photograph from the sold sub-types. Any "error" or "inverted overprint" 2017 surcharge
   should be presumed leaked waste absent provenance.
4. **The 2020 miniature sheet's reported 300-copy printing** makes it trivially worth forging.

### Verdict for the database

South Sudan is **not** a wallpaper issuer in the IGPC/Stamperija sense (no agency, no topical
flood, small genuinely national programme) — **but its philatelic record is contaminated at the
edges by unissued stock, printer's waste and unverified recent material**, and it sits on the
UPU's pending-denouncement list. Model it as **low-volume, high-uncertainty**, not as
high-volume junk. The two are different failure modes and want different handling.

## 8. Image sourcing

- **Wikimedia Commons: zero.** `Category:Stamps of South Sudan` returns **404** — the category
  does not exist. A media search for "South Sudan stamp" returns passport entry stamps, maps and
  other countries' stamps. **There is no reusable South Sudan stamp image on Commons today.**
- **Copyright status is genuinely murky, and murky is not the same as free.**
  - **South Sudan is not a party to the Berne Convention** (nor, as of the most recent
    documentation found, to any copyright convention; it has been urged by UNESCO/WIPO to join
    WIPO, ARIPO and Berne, and an EU/UNESCO project has been drafting a national copyright
    policy). Non-membership means no *automatic* protection abroad — it does **not** mean the
    designs are public domain, and it does not settle whether a *photograph* of a stamp is
    reusable.
  - **Sudan-era material** (which is the only route to pre-2011 imagery) is the ordinary Sudan
    copyright question and mostly turns on age: the 1897 overprints and the 1898–1948 Camel
    Postman are old enough to be safe in most jurisdictions.
- **Practical conclusion:** for pre-2011, source under **Sudan / Anglo-Egyptian Sudan** and lean
  on the pre-1948 material. For post-2011, **there is currently no lawful free image source
  identified.** Original photography of physical stamps, or a licence, is the only clean path.
  Dealer and auction-site scans are not licensed for reuse regardless of the underlying design's
  copyright status.

## 9. Uncertain — flagged, not asserted

Generous, because South Sudan deserves it.

- **Whether Stanley Gibbons lists South Sudan at all**, and under what numbers. The 2013 *North
  East Africa* scope list omits it. Unresolved. **No SG number should be recorded.**
- **Michel numbers.** The country is within ÜK 4/1 Nordafrika's scope; no actual Michel number
  was seen.
- **The true Scott range.** Only Scott 1–2 are confirmed by name. Whether the 2012/13 birds are
  Scott 3–8, whether the withdrawn 2.50 gets a number or a footnote, and where the 2017
  surcharges and 2020 COVID set sit, are all unknown. Linn's confirms Scott *carries* the 2017
  overprints (12 values repriced for the 2021 edition) — **12 values is itself an unexplained
  figure**, since only five base stamps are described as surcharged; it may count sub-types.
- **Colnect's ~35** came from a search summary, not the page (Colnect serves an Anubis
  bot-challenge to automated fetch). StampWorld's **56** likewise was not read off the page
  directly — stampworld.com returned 403.
- **Whether Arabic appears on any South Sudan stamp.** Expected not to, on constitutional
  grounds. Not verified against an image.
- **The 2024–2025 issues** (13th Anniversary, Gandhi, Sporting Associations, definitives, UPU
  150) and a reported forthcoming **15th Anniversary, 9 July 2026, 8 stamps + MS**. Dates and
  existence come from aggregator listings only. **Not confirmed as postal issues.**
- **WNS participation.** Not established.
- **Exact 2012/13 issue date.** Sources say "delayed from the planned 9 July 2012" into 2013
  without pinning a day.
- **Whether any post office outside Juba ever sold these stamps.** Distribution "to multiple post
  offices" is claimed; the absence of genuine commercial covers is noted by collectors. Unresolved.
- **Postal-service continuity 2013–2018.** Civil war disruption is certain in outline; no source
  documents which offices closed, when, or whether inland mail continued.
- **Whether Southern Sudan (1972–83 or 2005–11) ever produced even local or provisional postal
  labels.** Searched, nothing found. Absence of evidence.
