# Timor-Leste

**Date:** 19 July 2026 · **Purpose:** base-catalogue dossier. Portuguese Timor + UNTAET +
Democratic Republic of Timor-Leste. One of the worst "hidden era" traps in the corpus: a
24-year block of this country's postal history is catalogued under **Indonesia** and is
invisible to any search for "Timor".

---

**Which I trust more:** Colnect, marginally — it separates the two eras cleanly at the country
level (363 vs 62), which matches how catalogues actually behave, and its Portuguese-colonies
coverage is unusually complete because Portuguese colonial issues are heavily keytype-driven
and easy to enumerate. StampWorld's "Timor 1884–2000" range is suspicious: **there were no
Portuguese Timor issues after 1976**, so the "2000" endpoint is StampWorld filing the two
UNTAET stamps under the *old* country heading. That is itself a useful data point — see §4.

⚠️ **The headline is not "how many", it is "which country".** The interesting number in this
dossier is **0** — the number of separate stamps issued for East Timor 1976–1999 — set
against roughly two decades of Indonesian issues that *are* this territory's postal history
and are catalogued elsewhere entirely.

**Scott:** in Scott, the stamps of independent East Timor are **numbered consecutively
following those of Timor, the former Portuguese colony** — one continuous run. The exact
current highest Scott number could not be established from any free source. **Do not guess it.**

## 2. First issue

### Portuguese Timor

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Date | **1885** (Scott-aligned sources) / **1884** (Wikipedia, postoveznamky) — ⚠️ see below |
| Catalogue no. | **Scott 1** = 5 reis black |
| What it was | **Stamps of MACAU, "Portuguese Crown" keytype, overprinted `TIMOR`** |
| Set size | Ten stamps in the first issue (values 5–300 reis) |
| Currency | **reis** |
| Perforations | Both 12½ and 13½ exist on the underlying Macau crown keytypes |
| Printer | Not established — Portuguese colonial keytypes of this period were produced in Lisbon (Casa da Moeda); **unverified for this specific overprint** |

⚠️ **1884 vs 1885 is a genuine, unresolved conflict, not a typo.** Wikipedia and
postoveznamky.sk both say "overprinted on stamps of Macau in **1884**". Big Blue (working
directly from Scott) says the first stamps arrived in **1885** and that the first issue was
ten overprinted Macau stamps; another source explicitly identifies **Scott 1 as an 1885 5-reis
black of Macau overprinted "Timor"**. The 1884 date is very likely the date of the *underlying
Macau crown keytype*, which is firmly 1884, being misread as the date of the Timor overprint.
**Encode 1885 with an 1884 alias, and flag it.**

⚠️ **A second conflict on the first Timor-specific design.** Wikipedia: "a definitive set was
issued in **1886**". Big Blue: the first issue for Timor proper was **1887**, ten stamps,
"King Luiz" embossed vignette. There is also a documented **1886 essay of the "TIMOR"
overprint**, which is a plausible source of the 1886 claim. Unresolved.

### Modern era

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| UNTAET first issue | **29 April 2000** — two stamps, no value indicated, red for domestic, blue for international |
| Inscription | `Timor Lorosae` / `UNTAET`, marked `Dom.` or `Int.` |
| ⚠️ Conflicting date | One source gives **20 April 2000**. The **29 April** date is corroborated by two independent sources *and* by the UNTAET first-day cover held on Wikimedia Commons. Use 29 April; alias 20 April. |
| Independent first issue | **20 May 2002** (independence day) |
| Printer | **Australia Post** — unusually well attested for a first issue |
| Subjects | Independence commemoration; President Xanana Gusmão; map |

## 3. Issuing periods

| Period | Issuing authority | Stamps? |
|---|---|---|
| 1866–1884 | Timor administered **under Macau** | No own stamps; Macau stamps |
| **1885–1942** | Portuguese Timor (Portuguese colony) | Yes — from `TIMOR` on Macau, then own keytypes |
| **Feb 1942 – Sep 1945** | **Japanese military occupation** | ⚠️ **No occupation issue verified** — see §7 |
| **Sep 1945 – 1975** | Portuguese Timor restored (colony, later "overseas province") | Yes |
| **28 Nov – 7 Dec 1975** | Democratic Republic of East Timor (FRETILIN UDI) | ⚠️ **No issue verified** — 9 days |
| **17 Jul 1976 – Oct 1999** | **Republic of Indonesia** (annexed as 27th province) | **None of its own — Indonesian stamps used** |
| **Oct 1999 – 20 May 2002** | **UNTAET** (UN Transitional Administration in East Timor) | **Yes — 2 stamps, 29 Apr 2000** |
| **20 May 2002 –** | **Democratic Republic of Timor-Leste** / Correios de Timor-Leste | Yes |

- Power was formally handed back by Japan to Governor **Manuel de Abreu Ferreira de Carvalho
  on 5 September 1945**.
- Correios de Timor-Leste joined the **UPU on 28 November 2003** — i.e. *after* the first
  independent issue, and UNTAET's own postal mandate came from the UPU with **CTT Correios
  de Portugal** assisting.

## 4. Predecessor / successor issuers — the catalogue-heading map

**This is the highest-value field in this dossier.**

| Era | Scott heading | Stanley Gibbons heading | Colnect | StampWorld |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1885–1976 | **Timor** | **Timor** (in *Portugal & Colonies*, 1st ed.) | **Timor** (363) | **Timor** |
| 1976–1999 | 🚨 **INDONESIA** | 🚨 **Indonesia** | Indonesia | Indonesia |
| 2000 (UNTAET) | continues **Timor** numbering | ⚠️ unverified | ⚠️ unverified | appears under **Timor** (range ends "2000") |
| 2002– | **East Timor**, numbered **consecutively after Timor** | **East Timor** (⚠️ placement in SG's Portugal & Colonies vs Part 1 unverified) | **East Timor** (62) | **Timor Leste** |

Aliases a catalogue or a collector will actually use:
`Timor` · `Portuguese Timor` · `Timor Português` · `East Timor` · `Timor-Leste` ·
`Timor Leste` · `Timor Lorosae` · `Osttimor` (Michel) · `Portugiesisch-Timor` (Michel)

**Predecessor issuer for the first issues: `Macau`.** The 1885 stamps *are* Macau stamps.
Any provenance edge for Timor must point at Macau, not at Portugal directly.

🚨 **The Indonesia trap.** 1976–1999 material — 24 years, the single longest continuous stretch
of this territory's modern postal history — carries **no Timor inscription at all** and sits
under Indonesia in every catalogue. A user searching "Timor" or "East Timor" sees a hole
between 1976 and 2000 and concludes no mail existed. It did. This needs a succession edge
`Portuguese Timor → Indonesia → Timor-Leste` with the middle node marked
*used-here-but-not-inscribed*, exactly like the German Marshall Islands → Nauru case.

⚠️ Note also that **Michel splits where Scott joins**: Michel treats *Portugiesisch-Timor* and
*Osttimor* as separate country listings with independent numbering, while Scott runs one
sequence across the 1976–2002 gap. A cross-reference table that assumes 1:1 country identity
will mis-join these.

## 5. Inscriptions

### Portuguese era

| Text | Notes |
|---|---|
| `TIMOR` | The overprint on the 1885 Macau crowns; thereafter the standard country inscription |
| `CORREIOS` | "Posts" — plural form used on colonial issues |
| `CORREIO` | ⚠️ **Singular** on the Ceres keytype, per the Ceres series documentation. Both forms must be aliases. |
| `REPUBLICA PORTUGUESA` | From 1910/1911 (republic); Ceres keytype carries it |
| `PORTUGAL` | Appears on the 1930s omnibus/common-design issues (e.g. the 1935–38 "Portugal" and "São Gabriel" designs) |
| `IMPERIO COLONIAL PORTUGUES` | ⚠️ Standard on 1930s Portuguese colonial common designs — **not individually verified for Timor**, treat as probable not proven |

**Currency inscriptions, in order:**

| Period | Unit |
|---|---|
| 1885 – c.1894 | **reis** (`RS`) |
| c.1894 – 1958 | **avos / patacas** (100 avos = 1 pataca) — pataca was the unit of account 1894–1958 |
| 1959 – 1976 | **centavos / escudos** (100 centavos = 1 escudo; replaced pataca at 5.6 esc = 1 pataca) |

⚠️ The 1894 avo/pataca start is inferred from the *currency* history, not from a stamp.
The exact issue on which avos first appear on a Timor stamp is **not verified**.

### Japanese occupation
**No inscription established.** No verified occupation overprint — see §7.

### UNTAET (2000)
`Timor Lorosae` · `UNTAET` · `Dom.` (domestic) · `Int.` (international) · **no face value printed**.
*Timor Lorosae* is Tetum: "Timor of the rising sun" / "where the sun rises".

### Modern era (2002–)
`TIMOR LESTE` (the inscription actually reported on the 2002 issues) ·
`REPÚBLICA DEMOCRÁTICA DE TIMOR-LESTE` (⚠️ the constitutional long form — **not verified as
appearing on a stamp**; do not encode as an inscription until seen) ·
`Correios Timor Leste` (the operator name; ⚠️ likewise unverified as printed text).

**Bilingual practice:** Portuguese and Tetum are both official. `Timor Leste` and
`Timor Lorosae` are the Portuguese and Tetum names of the same country and both are attested
philatelically — the Tetum form on the UNTAET issue, the Portuguese form from 2002.
**Currency: US dollar / centavos** (Timor-Leste uses the USD, with locally minted centavo coins).
⚠️ Whether any Timor-Leste stamp carries a `$` or `centavos` denomination, or whether the
programme is service-indicator-only, is **not verified**.

## 6. Gaps

| Gap | Length | Cause |
|---|---|---|
| **1942–1945** | ~3 years | Japanese occupation. Portuguese administration interned at Liquiçá–Maubara. ⚠️ Whether *any* civil issue occurred is unverified; **no occupation issue found**. |
| **1976 – Apr 2000** | **~24 years** | Indonesian annexation. Indonesian stamps used; Dili cancels are Indonesian. **This is the big one.** |
| **2000 – May 2002** | ~2 years | UNTAET issued once (2 stamps) and did not issue again |
| **2002 – Nov 2005** | ~3.5 years | After the 2002 independence issue, **nothing until 4 definitives on 28 Nov 2005** |
| **2011 – present** | ⚠️ apparent, unproven | Documented issues thin out after the 2011 AICEP anniversary stamp. Colnect's 24 and StampWorld's range ending 2016 are consistent with a very sparse or dormant programme. **Not proven — treat as a research flag, not a fact.** |

Attested modern issues (Commonwealth Stamps Opinion, corroborated in part elsewhere):
2000 UNTAET ×2 · 2002 independence + Gusmão + map · 2005 4 definitives (flag) ·
2008 2 Nobel laureates · 2010 6 biodiversity (amphibians/reptiles) · 2011 AICEP anniversary.

## 7. Forgery and wallpaper risk

### Forgery — **HIGH for the classics**

- The **Macau "Portuguese Crown" keytypes are explicitly documented as having both reprints
  and forgeries**. Since Timor No. 1 *is* one of these with an added overprint, Timor's first
  issue inherits every forgery of the base stamp **plus** a forged-overprint risk on genuine
  base stamps — the classic two-layer problem.
- ⚠️ **The overprint is a single word in plain type.** `TIMOR` is about as easy to fake as an
  overprint gets, and the base Macau stamps are cheap and common. Assume any high-value
  1885 Timor overprint is suspect until expertised.
- **Perforation is a diagnostic, not a variety.** 12½ vs 13½ exist on the crown keytypes;
  a forger applying an overprint to the wrong perf creates a detectable fake.
- **Gum is not evidence.** Big Blue notes Timor stamps very frequently lack original gum
  because of tropical storage — so "no gum" is normal here and cannot be used as a red flag,
  and conversely fresh gum on a tropical-stored classic is itself suspicious.

### ⚠️ Japanese occupation — the myth to flag

**No Japanese occupation stamp issue for Portuguese Timor could be verified from any source.**
Wikipedia's postal-history article for Timor-Leste **does not mention Japanese occupation
stamps at all**. Searches for "Portuguese Timor" Japanese occupation overprints returned only
generic Japanese-occupation material for *other* territories (Dutch East Indies, North Borneo,
Malaya, Philippines) — all of which are themselves heavily forged. What *is* documented for
Timor 1942–45 is **military unit markings and censor handstamps**, i.e. markings on mail, not
stamps.

⚠️ **Treat any item offered as a "Japanese Occupation of Portuguese Timor" overprinted stamp
as unverified and probably fantasy.** This is the same shape as the Nauru case in the
exemplar. It is *not* proven that none exists — Scott could not be consulted — but the burden
of proof sits with the seller. **Do not encode a Japanese occupation issuing period with stamps.**

### 🚨 Wallpaper and bogus issues — two separate problems

**Problem 1 — bogus "East Timor" labels (severe, ongoing).**
Since **1999**, labels have been issued inscribed **`EAST TIMOR` / `PARA TIMOR`** which
"have nothing to do with the postal service on the island and are issued only for financial
gain." **East Timor appears on the UPU's list of postal administrations whose denouncements
of illegal stamps the UPU is helping to process.** These are illegals in the UPU's strict
sense: real country name, not produced by that country's postal administration, not valid
for postage anywhere.

**Problem 2 — Occussi-Ambeno (a cinderella, not a forgery).**
Wikimedia Commons' *Stamps of East Timor* category contains a **1976 "Occussi-Ambeno" stamp
of Teresa Teng**. Occussi-Ambeno is a real Timorese enclave (Oecusse) but the "Sultanate of
Occussi-Ambeno" is a **fantasy state invented by New Zealand artist Bruce Grenville / Bruce
Ronald Henderson**, issuing artistamps from 1968 onward, deliberately parodying countries that
commemorate everything. **Not fraudulent in intent, but absolutely not Timorese postage.**
🚨 It is mis-categorised on Commons under East Timor — meaning an automated Commons harvest
**will ingest fantasy material as genuine Timor stamps.** Blocklist `Occussi-Ambeno`,
`Occusi-Ambeno`, `Umm al Qitair`.

**Is modern Timor-Leste a wallpaper issuer? — NO. Plainly, emphatically no.**
Roughly **19–24 stamps in more than two decades**, described in the trade as
"pleasingly ultra-conservative", with a 3.5-year gap immediately after independence and
subjects that are genuinely national (Gusmão, the flag, the map, the two Nobel laureates,
local biodiversity). There is no IGPC/Stamperija-style topical programme. **The risk to a
Timor-Leste collector is the opposite of wallpaper: it is bogus third-party labels wearing
the country's name.**

**Does Portugal's colonial over-issuing reputation apply? — Only partially, and the charge is
"omnibus", not "wallpaper".**
Timor participated in the Portuguese colonial common-design/omnibus system — the 1898 Vasco
da Gama series (issued for Portugal and its colonies including Timor) and the 1930s designs.
Scott gives Portugal & Colonies its own **Common Design Types** section, alongside Europa,
France and the British Commonwealth — which is the catalogue's own acknowledgement of how
much repeated design there is. But omnibus issues were postally valid, ordinary-postage
stamps sold at face; that is a *dullness* complaint, not a legitimacy one. ⚠️ **The specific
claim that Portugal's colonial administration was criticised for excessive issues in the
1930s–60s did not survive checking** — searches for that criticism returned nothing;
what exists is neutral coverage of the omnibus *practice*. Do not assert it.

## 8. Image sourcing

⚠️ **The brief's premise that "Wikimedia Commons currently has none" is wrong.** Commons has
material — just very little.

| Commons category | Files |
|---|---|
| `Category:Stamps of Portuguese Timor` | **16** — subcats: 1898 (10), 1910 (4), 1933 (2) |
| `Category:Stamps of East Timor` | **5** direct + 2 subcats (`by year`, `Stamps of Portuguese Timor`) |
| Named files | UNTAET postage stamps · UNTAET first day cover · Timor Leste stamp type 1 / 1aa · Nicolau dos Reis Lobato · ⚠️ 1976 Occussi-Ambeno (**exclude — fantasy**) |

**Realistic reusable yield: ~20 images, of which ~16 are pre-1940 Portuguese colonial.**
Against ~400 catalogued majors that is **~5% coverage**, and it is heavily skewed to three
years (1898, 1910, 1933).

⚠️ A raw Commons *search* for "Timor stamp" returns ~93,700 results. **That number is
worthless** — it is matching passport/entry/exit stamps, rubber stamps, and generic Timor
imagery. Only the curated categories should be harvested.

**Copyright:**
- **Portugal: life of author + 70 years** for economic rights; moral rights perpetual.
  Portugal additionally has a **retroactive rule putting all photographs taken up to
  30 June 1970 in the public domain** (Decree-Law 334/97). The pre-1940 Timor issues are
  very likely PD by age, but ⚠️ **a stamp is generally an artistic work, not a photograph**,
  so the 1970 photo rule may not carry it — the life+70 clock runs from the designer's death,
  and **Portuguese colonial keytype designers are largely unattributed**, which makes the
  term unresolvable in principle. Commons resolves this case by case per file.
- **Timor-Leste's own copyright term was not established.** No source found. Treat all
  2002+ Timor-Leste designs as **in copyright** by default.
- **UNTAET stamps: unresolved and genuinely unusual.** These were issued by a UN transitional
  administration acting as the territory's postal authority — **not** by the UN Postal
  Administration (UNPA), which issues its own USD/CHF/EUR stamps. So the familiar "UN stamps"
  copyright position may not apply. ⚠️ No source found stating who holds copyright in the
  UNTAET designs. **Do not assume UN works are public domain — they are not.**

**Other candidate sources:** the Smithsonian National Postal Museum has a Timor-Leste
exhibition page (licence terms unchecked). Colnect / StampWorld / Scott images are **not**
reusable. Colnect and StampWorld both returned 403 / bot-challenge to automated fetch.

## 9. Uncertain — flagged, not asserted

- **1884 vs 1885** for the first issue. Both are in circulation from reputable sources.
  Probably an 1884 base stamp / 1885 overprint conflation, but **not proven**.
- **1886 vs 1887** for the first Timor-specific (King Luiz) definitive set. An 1886 *essay*
  of the TIMOR overprint exists and may be the source of the confusion.
- **20 vs 29 April 2000** for the UNTAET issue. 29 April is better supported.
- **Printer of the 1885 overprint** — not established. Lisbon Casa da Moeda is likely for the
  Macau base stamps; who applied the TIMOR overprint, and where, is unknown to me.
- **Current highest Scott number for East Timor.** Scott's continuous numbering across the
  Portuguese/independent boundary is documented; the actual number is not. No free source.
- **Stanley Gibbons numbers — none obtained.** SG *Portugal & Colonies* (1st ed., 2018-ish)
  covers Timor, but no SG number for any Timor stamp was recoverable from free sources.
  Even the question of **where SG files independent East Timor** (Portugal & Colonies volume,
  or a general Part) is unverified.
- **Michel numbers — none obtained.** The Michel split into *Portugiesisch-Timor* /
  *Osttimor* is inferred from Michel's general practice, not confirmed against a Michel volume.
- **Whether the exact texts `REPÚBLICA DEMOCRÁTICA DE TIMOR-LESTE` or `Correios Timor Leste`
  appear on any stamp.** Plausible, unverified. Not encoded.
- **Whether any Timor-Leste stamp carries a USD/centavos denomination.**
- **Whether any stamps were issued during the 9-day 1975 FRETILIN republic.** Almost certainly
  not — 9 days is not enough — but no source explicitly rules it out. There are Indonesian
  commemoratives of the *annexation* (an FDC of 17 August 1976 for East Timor's accession to
  Indonesia), which are Indonesian issues, not Timorese.
- **Whether any 1942–45 Japanese occupation stamp exists at all.** Absence of evidence here
  is fairly strong (Wikipedia's postal-history article is silent) but is not proof.
- **The post-2011 issuing gap.** Consistent with the counts, but not confirmed.
- **Timor-Leste copyright term.**
- **Copyright holder of the UNTAET designs.**
